# BW-SOS + PulaSafe (Botswana)

> **Category:** Two complementary national safety platforms
> **Headquarters:** Gaborone, Botswana
> **Websites:** https://bw-sos.appstor.io · https://pulasafe.com · https://safebotswana.com

## Overview
Botswana is small enough that three national players have emerged with complementary positioning:
- **BW-SOS** (Smart Device Technologies) — TomTom-based countrywide emergency-response app routing to nearest private-security partner; auto-notifies police / fire / medical / roadside-assist / ambulance.
- **PulaSafe** — AI-powered emergency and disaster-management platform with SMS / WhatsApp / USSD / web / email multi-channel alerting and a centralised control room for situational awareness.
- **SafeBots** — modern policing citizen-app (SOS + dispatcher chat + officer dashboard) explicitly tailored for Botswana Police Service.

## Core Offering
- **BW-SOS** — pan-Botswana GPS-routed emergency dispatch via vetted private partner network; sign-up captures medical + medical-aid + vehicle data for first responders.
- **PulaSafe** — AI-driven early warning, real-time disaster mapping, multi-channel citizen reporting, government & telco integration.
- **SafeBots** — Citizen Portal + Police Dashboard + Patrol Officer App + dispatcher chat.

## Pricing
BW-SOS subscription model; PulaSafe — institutional deployment pricing; SafeBots — B2G / B2B partnership model with Botswana Police.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Multi-channel redundancy** (SMS / USSD / web / WhatsApp + AI) — uniquely hardened for low-connectivity Botswana.
- Government-tied (SafeBots) gives it credibility and reach.
- Country-coverage with vetted private responder chain (BW-SOS).
- Botswana is a relatively wealthy African market, premium tolerated.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- All three are **app-only** — no medical wearable.
- No fall detection, no HR / SpO₂.
- Botswana-only — no other African countries.
- No API / partner SaaS model.

## Target Customer
Botswana residents, private-security subscribers, Botswana Police Service, district councils, telcos.
