# Usalama / MULIKA Kenya

> **Category:** Citizen-driven security-reporting app + SMS panic channel
> **Headquarters:** Nairobi / Thika, Kenya
> **Website:** https://mulikakenya.co.ke

## Overview
Two Kenyan solutions bundled here because they share a crowd-reporting / state-security coordination model. **Usalama** (Swahili for "security") is a shake-activated citizen panic app built by university student Edwin Inganji after a 2013 robbery. **MULIKA Kenya / SMS 988** (Mulika = "to shine a light") is a national Ministry / Integrity Support Services (ISS) citizen intelligence-reporting system that forwards every SMS to five county security chiefs and supports anonymous multimedia tips (photos / audio / video). The two act as a **complaint + reporting layer on top of** (not a replacement for) the Kenyan 999 / 112 emergency lines.

## Core Offering
- **Usalama app:** shake three times, hold volume, or tap icon → dispatches police/fire/medical to GPS and notifies nearby Usalama users.
- **MULIKA Kenya app + SMS 988:** free SMS to 988 (number concealed) forwarded to County Commissioner, County Police Commander, County AP Commander, County DCI, NIS officer. Photo / audio / video attachments supported.
- Anonymous-by-default, public-portal alternative.

## Pricing
Free (Usalama free download; 988 SMS free).

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **National visibility**: every county security chief receives the report; effectively a private back-channel to the security apparatus.
- Anonymous-by-design — strong for witnesses / corruption reporting.
- The SMS 988 channel reaches feature-phone users.
- Free, frictionless adoption.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- **Reporting, not response**: there is no private ambulance / armed-response dispatch.
- No medical-grade wearable, no fall detection.
- Tied to Kenyan state security apparatus — political risk if the regime changes.
- Older apps (Usalama app last updated 2017); MULIKA more recent but still app-only.

## Target Customer
General Kenyan public, crime witnesses, vigilante communities, *Nyumba Kumi* neighbourhood-watch groups.
