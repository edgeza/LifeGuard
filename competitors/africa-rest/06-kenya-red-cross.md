# Kenya Red Cross Society (KRCS) App

> **Category:** Humanitarian emergency app + SMS early-warning system
> **Headquarters:** Nairobi, Kenya
> **Website:** https://www.kenyaredcross.org

## Overview
The KRCS app ("THE PULSE") launched in 2016 as a self-described "world's first humanitarian smartphone app" — twelve functions including emergency alerts, ambulance request (Eplus-911), blood donation sign-up, KRCS news, volunteer registration, training enrollment and membership management. The KRCS / Airtel / IFRC **Trilogy Emergency Relief Application (TERA)** adds geo-targeted mass-SMS early warnings (floods, disease outbreaks, fires). KRCS / Safaricom **M-Salama** (*600#) extends the same model over Safaricom SMS to ~99 % of Kenya.

## Core Offering
- **12-function humanitarian app:** emergency alerts, ambulance (Eplus), blood donor registry, news, volunteer, training, membership, merchandise, Boma Hotels booking.
- **TERA mass-SMS early-warning system** — geo-targeted disaster / health / safety alerts to Airtel customers.
- **M-Salama early-warning SMS service** via Safaricom *600# (Ksh.1/SMS).
- Coordination with national and county disaster-management committees.

## Pricing
Free app download (membership pricing Ksh 500/yr); M-Salama charged at Ksh 1 / SMS; ambulance via membership plan.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **National brand trust** — KRCS is the default humanitarian responder in Kenya, recognised everywhere.
- Reaches the **non-smartphone segment** via *600# USSD.
- Multi-channel: app + SMS + mass-broadcast.
- Established partnerships with Airtel, Safaricom, IFRC, Vodafone Foundation, Novartis.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- **Not personal-safety focused** — emergency alerts are public-broadcast / crowd-reported, not one-to-one panic.
- No fall-detection wearable, no medical sensors.
- No medical/allergy profile operator console in the same class as MyLifeLine's.
- App functionality is a "humanitarian welfare app" more than a personal SOS.

## Target Customer
General Kenyan public, KRCS members and donors, disaster-affected communities, blood donors, volunteers, training enrollees.
