# Pariwo (MaiGAD) — Nigeria

> **Category:** Smart-home + personal wearable safety alarm (SMS-based, with GPRS pendant)
> **Headquarters:** Nigeria
> **Website:** https://www.pariwo.ng

## Overview
Pariwo's thesis is "**neighbour-to-neighbour + police**" panic dispatch using SMS to a pre-configured rescue network of neighbours, family, friends, street patrollers and the Nigeria Police Force. The product family includes **MaiGAD** (smart-home alarm with PIR + panic + remote + 1,800 mAh 48-hr backup), **Mini GPRS PAS** (compact GPS + WiFi + LBS personal alarm pendant for women, students, children, elderly, special-needs users), **SaferCity App** (subscription panic app for GBV + medical), and **PariwoCam**.

## Core Offering
- **MaiGAD** — home alarm + motion sensor + remote + silent-panic + app integration; police/neighbour SMS fanout on intrusion.
- **Mini GPRS Personal Alarm** — pocket-sized, GPS + LBS + WiFi, 2-day battery, audio monitoring, panic button, geofence no-go areas, USB charge.
- **SaferCity App** — subscription panic + GBV support worker comms.
- Full community-policing-forging ("OPC") + neighbour chain integration.

## Pricing
**MaiGAD** hardware + monthly monitoring; **Mini GPRS** device + subscription; SaferCity App subscription (Google Play).

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Multi-layered strategy** (home + pendant + app) covers more scenarios than a panic-only app.
- SMS-to-neighbours-and-police fan-out is unique and well-suited to Nigerian streets where patrol density varies.
- NGO / community-policing alignment gives the brand social credibility.
- GBV focus is meaningful, with direct social-worker handoff.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- No medical-grade sensors (HR / SpO₂ / fall detection) on the pendant.
- Subscription model is subscription-only — no prepaid / no shelf-bought SKU outside the home unit.
- No documented API for partner security companies.
- No national-responder SLAs — relies on neighbours being awake.

## Target Customer
Nigerian homeowners, OPC / community-policing forums, women, students, GBV survivors, elderly, special-needs families.
