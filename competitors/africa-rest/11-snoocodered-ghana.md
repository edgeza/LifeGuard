# SnooCODE RED (Ghana National Ambulance Service)

> **Category:** National-ambulance dispatch + digital addressing (B2G / B2B, with consumer component)
> **Operator:** SnooCODE Ltd in partnership with Vodafone Ghana Foundation, Ghana National Ambulance Service (GNAS), EMSOG, French Development Agency, Embassy of Switzerland
> **Website:** https://snoocode.com/red

## Overview
SnooCODE RED is **Ghana's national ambulance dispatch technology**. It combines a digital-addressing system (every Ghanaian has a unique 8-character code) with a 24/7 control centre for the **Ghana National Ambulance Service (GNAS)**. Founded 2015 in partnership with Vodafone Ghana Foundation, it cut ambulance response times by ~56 % in trials, trained 400+ EMTs, and powers a **most-capable-hospital routing engine** built with EMSOG. Works offline — a critical factor in rural Ghana.

## Core Offering
- **Offline-capable digital addressing** — every citizen gets a SnooCODE; responders can locate them without GPS.
- **Ambulance dispatch console** — SnooCODE RED app on EMT handsets in Accra and Kumasi.
- **Most-capable-hospital routing** — given an emergency type, dispatcher sees the hospital with the right specialist / equipment.
- **SRCC (SnooCODE RED Control Centre)** modular platform — emergency, public-health, primary-healthcare, epidemiology.
- 99 % offline operation.

## Pricing
Free for citizens; B2G (GNAS) — funded by Vodafone Ghana Foundation, EMSOG, Swiss / AFD donors, Mastercard Foundation.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Ghana national-scale infrastructure** — embedded in the state ambulance service.
- Works without internet — unique advantage.
- Strong research evidence base (Injury / Elsevier 2025 study, LFR International partnership).
- Massive donor backing (Mastercard Foundation, AFD, Swiss, Vodafone).

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- **B2G / B2B** — not a personal consumer panic product.
- Ghana-only.
- No personal wearable / no consumer panic app.
- No private-responder SLA.

## Target Customer
Ghana National Ambulance Service, regional hospitals, EMSOG (Emergency Medicine Society of Ghana), rural clinics, district health authorities.
