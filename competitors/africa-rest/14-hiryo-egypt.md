# Hiryo — Egypt

> **Category:** Personal & family safety ecosystem (app + 1st MENA legal personal-safety hardware)
> **Headquarters:** Cairo, Egypt
> **Website:** https://www.hiryo.app

## Overview
Hiryo is Egypt's leading personal-safety startup, winner of the **African Startup of the Year** at the Global Startup Awards and a UNFPA-backed women's-safety initiative. It combines a circle-of-trust mobile app with **Fly**, the first legal personal safety alarm (a pocket-sized pull-pin alarm louder than a fire alarm) sold across MENA. The roadmap is "fully integrated personal safety ecosystem in MENA: app + Fly hardware + eventually integrated Fly-with-SOS."

## Core Offering
- **Hiryo app** — real-time location sharing, 1-tap SOS alerts to user's "circle of trust," silent distress trigger.
- **Fly Personal Alarm** — pocket-sized, TSA-approved, EGP 1,0xx, louder than a fire alarm; first legal personal-safety alarm in MENA.
- **Roadmap:** Fly hardware integrated with Hiryo app to send live location + SOS to the user's circle.
- Target also includes women, students, families, expats.

## Pricing
App free; **Fly ≈ EGP 1,000+** (one-off hardware).

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Award-winning brand and ecosystem play** — only African competitor explicitly building a *hardware + app + ecosystem* comparable to MyLifeLine's DNA.
- **Cultural legitimacy** — Fly is the only legal self-defence alarm in MENA, no pepper-spray alternative.
- Strong backing — UNFPA Arab States, African Startup of the Year, Microsoft Africa Advisory tie-ins.
- Built for MENA cultural / safety norms (circle of trust, not always armed response).

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- Fly is a passive **audible alarm** — no GPS, no cellular SOS, no two-way voice, no medical sensors.
- App is family-share, **not device-to-control-room** — no dispatch chain.
- No elderly fall detection, no HR / SpO₂ / medical platform.
- Egypt / MENA focus, not pan-African.

## Target Customer
MENA women, students, families, runners, expats, female-headed households, NGO programmes.
