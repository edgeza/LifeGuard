# TravelSafe SOS (Southern & East Africa)

> **Category:** Travel-emergency coordination app (no consumer panic)
> **Headquarters:** South Africa (controls operations across Southern & East Africa)
> **Website:** https://travelsafesos.com

## Overview
TravelSafe SOS is a South-African-built travel-safety app founded in 2003, now covering **18 African countries**: South Africa, Namibia, Botswana, Mozambique, Zimbabwe, Malawi, Tanzania, Zanzibar, Kenya, Uganda, Rwanda, Zambia, Mauritius, Seychelles, Madagascar, Lesotho, Eswatini. It is sold to **tourists, tour operators and travel-insurers** rather than residents — a different buyer from MyLifeLine — but is the dominant pan-African medical/security evacuation-coordination app and a credible partner target.

## Core Offering
- **One-tap SOS** connects to 24/7 control centre with operator phone-back, triage, dispatch into vetted local ambulance / paramedic / private-security / air-evacuation network.
- **Automatic alerts** to travel insurer + travel agent + next-of-kin on activation.
- **Offline-ready GPS logging** when entering no-signal regions; re-sync on signal return.
- **Medical profile** stored in app (allergies, blood type, conditions) — handed to responders.
- **Embassy and consulate contact cards** for each country.
- ZMK 7,200 / 12-month subscription equivalent; R350 once-off plus monthly / annual options.

## Pricing
**R350 (≈US$20) once-off, or monthly / annual subscription.** Charged in ZAR.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Pan-African footprint, 18 countries** — the only competitor at this scale across southern + east Africa.
- Operator call-back within minutes, real onboarding of air-ambulance, lodge & park-authority partners.
- Strong tour-operator and insurance-distribution channels (Safari companies pre-purchase for guests).
- Built specifically for **safari / mountain / dive / remote-rural incidents** — different threat model to a city panic button.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- **Tourist / B2B-sale oriented**, not a local resident subscription → not a head-to-head consumer competitor.
- No wearable, no medical sensors.
- No elderly fall-detection or daily-wear use case.
- South-African-domiciled dispatch model (smoothest in SADC, harder further north).

## Target Customer
African safari / island / mountaineering travellers, tour operators, lodges, travel insurers, expats.
