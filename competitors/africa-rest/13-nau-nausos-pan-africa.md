# NauNauSOS — Pan-African

> **Category:** Consumer personal-safety app (white-label to many African countries)
> **Headquarters:** Pan-African (country-specific landing pages: GH, EG, NG, KE, ET, UG …)
> **Website:** https://naunausosapp.com

## Overview
NauNauSOS is a "build once, theme per country" personal-safety app with dedicated pages for at least **Ghana, Egypt, Nigeria, Kenya, Ethiopia, Uganda** and more — each pitched at students, families and professionals in the named country's largest cities. Strategy is consistent: instant SOS, live location sharing, family connect, journey tracker, push notifications.

## Core Offering
- **Instant SOS** — one-tap distress message to chosen contacts with live GPS.
- **Live location sharing** for commutes, campus walks, travel.
- **Family** connect — multi-user account with shared map.
- **Push notifications** during distress.
- **Specific student-protection pitch** in each country (UG → KNUST / Makerere / Cape Coast; EG → Cairo / Alexandria / Giza).
- **Flexible One-A-Day Plans** for Accra professionals, etc.

## Pricing
Per-country monthly plans; "One-A-Day" plans and family bundles.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Multi-country footprint** in Rest-of-Africa; English + Portuguese/Swahili page layouts.
- Student-customer-base playbook — clear undergrad / family-on-campus pitch.
- "One-A-Day Plans" priced for emerging-market spending patterns.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- **App-only** — no wearable, no medical sensors.
- No fallback SMS / USSD path on low-connectivity days.
- No dispatch network; relies on contacts responding.
- Brand thinner than the country-leaders in each market.

## Target Customer
Students (UG, KNUST, Cape Coast, Makerere, GA universities), families, commuters, professionals, expats.
