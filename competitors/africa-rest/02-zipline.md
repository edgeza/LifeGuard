# Zipline

> **Category:** Autonomous drone medical-logistics (not a direct panic-button competitor — adjacent)
> **Headquarters:** Half Moon Bay, California, USA · Africa HQ: Kigali, Rwanda
> **Website:** https://www.zipline.com

## Overview
Zipline began operations in Rwanda in 2016 as the world's first national-scale drone medical-delivery service. It now operates across Rwanda, Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya and Côte d'Ivoire, has signed a continental MoU with Africa CDC, and has delivered >1 million packages. It is the dominant drone-based *medical supply chain* in sub-Saharan Africa — **not a personal emergency-response competitor to MyLifeLine** — but it is the strategic benchmark for "tech-enabled African emergency infrastructure" and an obvious partner or acquisition target.

## Core Offering
- **On-demand drone delivery** of blood, vaccines, antivenom, rabies immunoglobulin, malaria treatments, and lab samples to hospitals/health posts.
- **~15 m drop** within 80 km of a Zipline distribution centre — used for postpartum-hemorrhage blood, childhood emergencies, snakebite, dog-bite rabies.
- **WhatsApp / SMS ordering** by health workers; pre-programmed GPS routes approved by Rwandan Civil Aviation Authority.
- Operates 24/7, all-weather; payload ~2 kg / fixed-wing.

## Pricing
Per-country fixed monthly rate to the Ministry of Health (not consumer pricing). ~2 kg packages, sub-hour delivery across most of Rwanda.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- Strategic infrastructure partner for emergency medical logistics in Africa.
- Massive brand and proof points: Rwanda PPH mortality −51 %, Ghana missed-vaccination sessions −44 %.
- Direct route into national health ministries — partner-of-record.
- Africa CDC MoU positions Zipline as a continental layer, not just Rwanda.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- **B2B / B2G only** — no direct-to-consumer product, no personal panic app, no wearable.
- Hardware and routing are pre-programmed for fixed locations; **cannot currently fly to ad-hoc GPS coordinates** of an injured citizen.
- No lone-worker / personal emergency-response function whatsoever.

## Target Customer
National Ministries of Health, hospitals, clinics, public-health programmes, Africa CDC, pharmaceutical distributors.
