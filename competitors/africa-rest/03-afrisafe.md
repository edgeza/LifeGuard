# AfriSafe

> **Category:** Community-powered personal safety app
> **Headquarters:** Nairobi, Kenya (launch market)
> **Website:** https://afrisafe.app

## Overview
AfriSafe is a pan-African community-safety network mobile app built for women, children and families. It launched in Kenya and is rolling out across the continent. The pitch: turn trusted contacts, plus a network of "community guardians", into a personal safety net — get help in ~60 s rather than waiting for unreliable state emergency lines. Notable for being **SMS-fallback-capable** so it works on 2G where other apps die.

## Core Offering
- **One-tap SOS** that alerts all emergency contacts with live GPS location over SMS (not just push notifications) — works on 2G.
- **Voice trigger "Nisaidie!" (Help me!)** for hands-free activation.
- **Stealth mode** — disguised as a calculator app; type `911=` to unlock (safety against abuser discovery).
- **Journey tracking + safe-space mapping** (police stations, hospitals, shelters, safe houses).
- Community guardian concept — nearby users opt-in to receive nearby SOS alerts and respond.

## Pricing
Free download; freemium model with paid Premium tier (community-guardian responder training, expanded safe-space data).

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Pan-African brand and continent-first positioning** — explicitly designed to be portable across Kenya → Nigeria → SA.
- SMS-fallback is a real differentiator for low-connectivity African markets.
- Stealth / disguise-UI is rare and meaningful for GBV victims.
- Community-guardian model scales without building a private armed-response network (which would be uneconomical outside ZA).

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- App-only — no medical-grade wearable, no fall detection, no HR / SpO₂.
- No dedicated dispatch network or vetted responder chain; relies on family + community members, which is fragile at scale.
- No documented medical/allergy profile workflow for first responders.

## Target Customer
Women, families, students, domestic-violence survivors, anyone walking alone at night in African cities.
