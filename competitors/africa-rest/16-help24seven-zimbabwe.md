# Help24Seven (Zimbabwe)

> **Category:** Consumer panic / emergency-services app
> **Headquarters:** Harare, Zimbabwe (founded by Rodney Beadon, partnered with J&P Security)
> **Website:** https://help24seven.co.zw

## Overview
Help24Seven is **Zimbabwe's only consumer emergency + specialist-services panic-button app**, run in partnership with J&P Security, the country's leading private security firm. Two-button interface: a **three-second hold** for emergency (operator calls back and dispatches); a **five-second hold** for silent panic (no caller-back, immediate covert security dispatch). Beyond panic, the app routes to ambulance, fire, medical, vets, and home-services categories.

## Core Offering
- **EMERGENCY button** (3-s hold) — call-back, dispatch, follow-through until service arrives.
- **PANIC button** (5-s hold) — silent alert with no callback, immediate covert dispatch.
- **Specialist categories** — ambulance, fire, security, medical, vet, handyman, etc.
- **Nationwide coverage via J&P Security** and partner network.
- Android primarily; iOS in final approval stages.

## Pricing
Subscription-based; bundle with J&P Security monitoring.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- Direct partnership with Zimbabwe's **largest private security firm** gives genuine nationwide response.
- **Silent panic** option is critical for intrusion scenarios (no caller-back means no attacker learns help is coming).
- Two-tier button UX (Emergency vs Panic) is unusually well-considered.
- National coverage gives it a monopoly in Zimbabwe.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- **Zimbabwe-only**, partner-locked to J&P.
- App-only — no medical pendant, no fall detection, no medical sensors.
- No API / Webhooks for partner integration.
- Brand maturity is recent (founded by individual entrepreneur).

## Target Customer
Zimbabwean residents, J&P Security subscribers, businesses, families.
