# AURA (Kenya)

> **Category:** Response-network platform (B2B + white-label + lone-worker)
> **Headquarters:** Johannesburg, ZA · Operations: Kenya, ZA, UK, US
> **Website:** https://www.aurasos.com/en-ke

## Overview
AURA's Kenya operation (AURA Holdings Kenya, registered as a separate entity with PSRA-licensed private-security partners) is the East-African mirror of its South African platform. It is the leading tech-enabled emergency-response network across Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu and major industrial hubs, and is the dispatch backbone for many of the consumer safety apps being launched in the Kenyan market (911 Group, BM Security SmartPanics, dating-app and ride-hailing integrations). It also markets a dedicated **lone-worker / telematics safety solution** to oil-and-gas, mining, logistics, security, utilities and field-service employers.

## Core Offering
- **One-tap SOS via smartphone or wearable** routed to AURA's 24/7 Nairobi control room.
- **PSRA-registered vetted responder network** — sub-15-minute average response in urban Kenyan metros.
- **Lone-worker / B2B module** — SOS, man-down, no-motion, gas detection, journey/trip monitoring, two-way voice, mass-notification broadcast.
- **White-label apps and APIs** — partner telcos, banks, ride-hailing apps and security companies can re-skin the safety experience.
- **Telematics-safety integration** — pairs with in-vehicle devices (including Track24-derived units) for fleet drivers in high-risk zones.

## Pricing
Not publicly published; B2B subscription per-seat / per-vehicle with API/white-label commercial model. Sub-15 min urban SLA.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Network effect in Kenya** — only operator with a vetted, near-nationwide physical responder pool.
- **Sub-15-minute boots-on-the-ground response** that any GPS-only app cannot match.
- White-label/API platform lets AURA embed inside any bank/insurer product, threatening MyLifeLine's partnership moat.
- Lone-worker / B2B module is more mature than MyLifeLine's current B2B offering for fleets and field staff.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- **Not wearable-first** — its DNA is dispatch + smartphone app. Wearables come via partner integration, not a curated medical-grade EV06/EV07-style lineup.
- No fall-detecting pendant with HR / SpO₂ / two-way voice shipped under its own brand.
- Consumer brand in Kenya is weaker than its ZA dominance — MyLifeLine has a credible story for elderly / medical niches it does not yet dominate.

## Target Customer
Banks, insurers, telcos, ride-hailing/dating apps, fleets, mining/oil-gas/utilities, security companies, NGOs with field staff, and (via white-label) the general public.
