# SafeNess (Morocco), Roro (Egypt) — North-Africa GBV / personal-safety apps

> **Category:** North-Africa personal-safety apps
> **Headquarters:** Morocco / Egypt
> **Websites:** SafeNess Google Play (app.safeness.safeness) · Roro on App Store (id1585189349)

## Overview
Two Morocco / Egypt apps share a similar "trusted-circle + voice evidence" model:
- **SafeNess** (CAWTAR) — also active in **Morocco** across 36+ regions; mobile app with anonymous location-sharing + automatic voice-recording as legal evidence for sexual-harassment / public-violence complaints. Tuned for cultural context of MENA.
- **Roro: Safety App + Magic SOS** (Egypt, also India / global) — patent-pending "missed-check-in triggers guardian alert" model. The user schedules check-ins; if missed, Roro pings guardians via WhatsApp with last-known location. Less about panic, more about silent intervention; zero ads, zero tracking, free.

## Core Offering
- **SafeNess** — trusted contacts, voice recording with time/GPS as evidence, free MENA rollout via CAWTAR.
- **Roro** — interval-based check-in; guardian alert if check-in missed; WhatsApp-based intervention; optional auto-arm when leaving predefined zones (home/office); last-known-only location storage; "passcode wrong = alert guardians."

## Pricing
Both free.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Lightweight, free MENA infrastructure** — public-sector backed (SafeNess via UNECA CAWTAR).
- Legal-evidence orientation (SafeNess) is credible for MENA legal context.
- Patent-pending missed-check-in logic in Roro is genuinely creative in a sea of panic-button apps.
- Privacy-first design in Roro (no ads, no tracking).

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- **No arm/dispatch chain** — family and friends only.
- No medical sensors, no fall detection.
- No wearable, no platform / API.
- No pan-African reach (Morocco-only for SafeNess; Egypt-born Roro is global).

## Target Customer
MENA women, students, expats, harassment / GBV survivors, parents of young adults abroad.
