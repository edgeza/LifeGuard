## Product lineup

- **997 Emergency Hotline** — free nationwide EMS dispatch; Arabic + English.
- **Asafny Mobile App** ("Save me") — launched 2017, smartphone-based emergency request. Supports non-Arabic speakers and deaf/mute users; saves patient medical history; live location sharing; chat-based communication with dispatch; nearest-health-facility lookup; relay to other relief agencies. iPhone integration auto-dials 997 with current location.
- **Ambulance Medical Coverage Service** — contracted ambulance coverage for events (SAHEM service) and individual subscribers.
- **Training Programs** — public first-aid, hospital-staff emergency training.
- **Volunteering Programs** — SRCA volunteer network for events and disasters.
- **Conference & Event Medical Coverage** — sports, Hajj, Umrah, national events.

## Differentiation

SRCA is the **sole government-authorised 997 EMS operator in Saudi Arabia** — there is no competitor to the 997 hotline for emergency medical dispatch. Asafny (launched 2017) is the only authorised mobile-app alternative to voice 997 calls, with specific features for deaf/mute users, non-Arabic speakers, and patient-medical-history storage. The combination is the closest thing to a free public "PERS" for Saudi citizens — but it requires the senior to be conscious, have a phone, and know to use the app.

## Strengths

- ✅ **Government monopoly on 997** — no private EMS can legally dispatch emergency ambulances in KSA.
- ✅ **Free at point of use** — no Saudi consumer will outcompete free emergency dispatch.
- ✅ **Asafny mobile app** — purpose-built for deaf/mute users, non-Arabic speakers, and smartphone-equipped citizens. Two-way communication with dispatch; live location; medical history.
- ✅ **459 branches nationwide** — including Hajj / Umrah coverage; the only EMS operator that reaches every Saudi administrative region.
- ✅ **44-hospital integration in Eastern Province alone** — pre-arrival hospital notification cuts triage time at the ED.
- ✅ **Saudi Vision 2030 alignment** — Asafny launched as part of the National Transformation Program; sustained government backing.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ **Public-service KPIs, not concierge.** Average 997 response time is the standard Saudi target (~8-15 min); not a premium 15-min private guarantee.
- ❌ **Asafny requires smartphone + consciousness.** A senior who falls and is unconscious + has no smartphone cannot use Asafny. PERS pendant + fall detection is the gap.
- ❌ **No consumer PERS / wearable subscription** — Saudi citizens cannot subscribe to a $30-$80/mo pendant service from SRCA. The private market must fill this.
- ❌ **No caregiver/family app** — Asafny is patient-to-dispatch, not family-to-elder.
- ❌ **97% of calls answered, but 87% accepted for transport** (vs. 56% for Asafny) — phone 997 is still the primary channel for serious emergencies.
- ❌ **Government app, not consumer product** — UX priorities are dispatch-grade, not elderly-friendly.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **The Saudi private PERS market is wide open.** SRCA + Asafny cover emergency dispatch but ship zero subscription pendants, smartwatches, or caregiver apps. The Saudi private-sector opportunity (Me'kaaz, Vitalis, Zard, Manzil-from-UAE) is fragmented and pre-scale.
- **Whitelabel Asafny integration.** Trigger Asafny dispatch automatically from MyLifeLine pendant SOS + fall-detection alerts — without the senior needing to tap the app. This is the highest-value partnership in KSA.
- **SFDA-registered wearable is the wedge.** Localise the pendant hardware for SFDA registration (Me'kaaz already did this); partner with SRCA for fall-back dispatch.
- **Saudi adult-child buyer is the primary GTM.** Adult children of Saudi seniors are tech-comfortable, English-Arabic bilingual, and willing to pay $30-$80/mo for peace of mind. The Saudi expatriate market is even larger (no government-paid PERS exists for expats).
