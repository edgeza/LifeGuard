## Differentiation

MobiHealth is **the first fully integrated telehealth platform with multi-country (UK/Nigeria/UAE) registration + B2B/B2C/B2G/SaaS/white-label distribution** — at $5-$10/mo subscription pricing that's an order of magnitude below US PERS. Its Union Bank of Nigeria partnership (2023) is the template for B2B2C fintech-bundle distribution that could be replicated in the UAE with ADCB, FAB, or Emirates NBD.

## Strengths

- ✅ **First-mover integrated telehealth platform** in Africa with multi-country registration (UK + Nigeria + UAE).
- ✅ **BCG-validated feasibility study** (USTDA-sponsored) — institutional credibility that bootstrapped startups lack.
- ✅ **Five-segment distribution** — D2C + B2B + B2G + SaaS + white-label = diversified GTM.
- ✅ **Union Bank of Nigeria partnership** — template for B2B2C fintech-bundle distribution.
- ✅ **$5-$10/mo subscription pricing** — radically lower than US PERS; serves the mass-market African segment.
- ✅ **International doctor network** — US/UK/Germany/Israel-trained physicians accessible via app.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ **Not a PERS / wearable subscription brand.** No pendant, no smartwatch, no fall detection. A senior who falls needs to be conscious and have a smartphone + data plan.
- ❌ **Africa-centric** — UAE presence is corporate / partnership-stage, not consumer-scale. UAE regulatory + consumer brand recall is weak.
- ❌ **No caregiver / family app** for ongoing visibility.
- ❌ **Subscription price ($5/mo) is too low** for a 24/7 trained-care-specialist + ambulance-dispatch service — cannot fund the operations layer.
- ❌ **No published UAE pricing** — comparison shoppers cannot price-shop MobiHealth in MENA.
- ❌ **Telemedicine licensing varies by country** — multi-jurisdiction regulatory risk.
