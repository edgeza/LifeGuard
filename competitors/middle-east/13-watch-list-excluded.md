## 1. Life Guardian UAE — ❌ Not a UAE company

**Actual entity:** Life Guardian is a product of **GlobalTek Security Inc.** (Canada), sold in CAD and USD. The website (lifeguardianmobile.com/cad/ and /usd/) markets a senior PERS pendant with 24/7/365 ULC-certified monitoring, fall detection, GPS wandering alert, two-way voice, starting at **$29.99/mo (US) or $39.99/mo (Canada)** with a $39.99 one-time setup fee. The brand supplies Bayshore Healthcare, which services 350,000+ seniors in North America.

**Why excluded:** There is **no evidence of a UAE-licensed Life Guardian entity**. The name in the brief is almost certainly a confusion with another brand. Possible candidates: the Gilani Mobility "Emergency Vital Call Fall Alarm" (UAE mobility retailer — see Watch List #4) or the dozens of Chinese 4G GPS tracker brands sold on Amazon.ae.

**Watch if:** GlobalTek Security Inc. announces a UAE / GCC distribution partnership.

## Implications for the dossier

- The Middle East **subscription PERS market is structurally underserved**. The closest direct competitors are Saudi-built Me'kaaz (profile 09), US-built Vitalis (watch only), and Chinese-built GPS smartwatches sold on Amazon.ae.
- Every major home-healthcare / hospital-network player (Manzil, FRH, Medcare, Amana, SEHA, Saudi German, NMC) is **complementary, not competitive** — they provide clinical home visits + chronic care, not 24/7 monitoring + fall detection + trained-care specialists.
- The government EMS players (SRCA + Asafny in KSA, National Ambulance in UAE, SEHA in Abu Dhabi) are **free public backstops** that MyLifeLine must integrate with, not compete with.
- The platform plays (MobiHealth, Okadoc) are **SaaS partnerships**, not direct competitors.
