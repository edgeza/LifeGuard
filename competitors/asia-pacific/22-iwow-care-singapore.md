## Differentiation

iWOW Care is the only Singapore LoRaWAN-based panic-alert infrastructure provider for elderly public-housing estates, deployed across 16 Singapore townships since November 2019. Selling point: municipal-scale B2G deployment, LoRaWAN long-range battery-powered buttons, cloud-based monitoring.

## Strengths

- ✅ LoRaWAN long-range + low-power = superior battery life vs cellular.
- ✅ Deployed at scale (16 townships; 10,000 households planned).
- ✅ B2G/B2B business model — direct municipal / housing-association channel.
- ✅ Cloud-based monitoring — easy integration with municipal call centres.
- ✅ Operating since 2019 — operational track record.
- ✅ Singapore-grade infrastructure (governmental compliance).

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ No fall detection — disqualifies fall-risk seniors.
- ❌ No GPS tracking — fixed LoRaWAN coverage only.
- ❌ B2G-only — no D2C consumer channel.
- ❌ Singapore-only deployment; no ASEAN export yet.
- ❌ No caregiver / family app.
- ❌ Technology-stack (LoRaWAN) limits smart-home integrations.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Consumer D2C channel.** iWOW Care is B2G-only; MyLifeLine's B2C pendant is the complement for the cohort that doesn't qualify for municipal deployment.
- **Mobile pendant.** iWOW Care is in-home LoRaWAN only; MyLifeLine EV07BA covers outdoor.
- **Cross-border ASEAN export.** iWOW Care has no ASEAN export; MyLifeLine's B2B SaaS could white-label to Malaysian / Indonesian municipalities.
- **Caregiver app integration.** iWOW Care's monitoring is responder-only; MyLifeLine's caregiver dashboard would add family visibility.
