## Product lineup

- **Gateway** — main unit installed in living space; emergency button auto-dials 119 (transmits resident name + phone + address).
- **Fire detector (화재감지기)** — automatic 119 fire report.
- **Emergency call device (응급호출기)** — bathroom or bedroom; emergency button auto-dials 119.
- **Activity sensor (활동량감지기)** — measures movement, heart rate, respiration; flags "fallen or unconscious" to emergency-management personnel.
- **Radar sensor** — bed-exit detection; activity monitoring; combined with activity sensor for unconscious-detection.
- **Entry sensor** — outdoor exit detection; flags prolonged absence from home.
- **Mobile App** — caseworker + family notifications.

## Differentiation

Korea 119 Ansim Call is the only nationwide government-funded senior PERS in APAC that integrates directly with the 119 fire-and-rescue dispatch system. Selling point: zero cost for the user, 119-direct dispatch (no third-party call centre), activity/radar-sensor anomaly detection (no wearable required).

## Strengths

- ✅ Fully government-funded — zero cost to user.
- ✅ Direct 119 dispatch — bypasses any third-party call centre.
- ✅ Multi-sensor detection (fire + activity + radar + entry) — high signal.
- ✅ National-scale programme — political stability.
- ✅ Expansion to elderly couples + grandchild households (2025 scope-widening).
- ✅ SK Telecom AI integration ("긴급 SOS" via NUGU AI speaker) — modern UX layer.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ Means-tested — eligible only for seniors 65+ living alone (recently widened to couples / grandchild households in 2025).
- ❌ Waiting lists of 1–3 years in some municipalities due to limited installation capacity ⚠️.
- ❌ In-home only — no mobile GPS pendant.
- ❌ Korean language only — limits migrant-elderly access.
- ❌ No clinical triage layer — 119 dispatches paramedics; no nurse/GP escalation.
- ❌ Low English documentation — limits export of the model.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Model export.** Korea's 119 Ansim Call is a credible blueprint for B2G export to other APAC jurisdictions (Singapore HDB AAS, Japan municipal Mimamori, Thailand municipal Sukjai).
