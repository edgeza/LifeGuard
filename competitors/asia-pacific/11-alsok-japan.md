## Differentiation

ALSOK is Japan's #1 home-security company by customer count (530,000+) and the keiretsu-affiliated incumbent for elderly-monitoring via the Mimamori Support tier of its home-security bundle. Selling point: guardman dispatch as standard (not "we'll call an ambulance"), nurse consultation via ALSOK Healthcare Centre, and J-Alert earthquake/tsunami relay for elderly living alone.

## Strengths

- ✅ Largest Japanese home-security customer base (530,000+) — built-in cross-sell to existing customers.
- ✅ Owned guard-centre network (53+) — fastest physical-guardman dispatch in Japan.
- ✅ ALSOK Healthcare Centre (nurses + social workers on call 24/7) — clinical escalation.
- ✅ Bundles fire, gas-leak, intruder, life-rhythm, heat-stroke, dementia-wander, J-Alert — single-bundle safety story.
- ✅ Five-Diamond-equivalent guardman training (ALSOK介助 internal certification).
- ✅ Keiretsu backing — institutional credibility.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ Monthly cost JPY 1,870–8,668 ≈ US$13–US$60 — substantially higher than MyLifeLine wholesale (R39-R180 ≈ US$2-US$10) per device.
- ❌ No fall detection in core pendant (must rely on life-rhythm sensor proxy).
- ❌ Mobile pendant is home-only; outdoor wearable is separate SKUs.
- ❌ 5-year contracts typical (foreign customers unaccustomed to this).
- ❌ Caregiver app is mail-notification only — no modern dashboard.
- ❌ Foreign-language support limited to English call-centre for inbound corporate.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Disrupt on contract length and price.** ALSOK's 5-year + JPY 1,870–8,668/mo bundle is heavy friction; MyLifeLine's no-contract / R39-R180 wholesale model is the opposite end.
