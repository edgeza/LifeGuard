## How to read this summary

The Asia-Pacific PERS / connected-safety market in 2026 is structurally different from the US/UK/Canada markets MyLifeLine likely originated in. Five structural facts dominate the strategy:

1. **There is no dominant pan-APAC subscription-PERS brand.** Every country has 1–3 local incumbents that don't export. The closest thing to a regional brand is **Tunstall** (UK-owned, AU/NZ operating), but its APAC reach is limited to Australia + NZ.
2. **Government / non-profit PERS is the dominant scale channel.** MePACS (AU, ~40k subs), SCHSA (HK, 111k cumulative), St John NZ (~30k subs), Korea 119 Ansim Call (11k+ households in SKT/SKT alone, nationwide programme), Singapore Red Cross HoME+, RDNS/Silver Chain (WA/SA) — every major APAC market has a state-subsidised or charity-funded baseline. These players compete on **trust + zero cost**, not on subscription.
3. **Security-company PERS (SECOM, ALSOK, ADT-Chubb) is the mass-market volume play in Japan + ANZ.** 5-year contracts + JPY 1,870–8,668/mo (SECOM/ALSOK) or A$29.99–A$41.99/mo (ADT Health) bundle PERS into home-security; cross-sell is the moat, not the pendant.
4. **Smartwatch OEM platform plays (Apple Watch, Samsung, Xiaomi Mi SOS, OPPO ColorOS)** route to emergency contacts + 999/911/119/120 — not a trained-care-specialist ARC. They are the zero-subscription disruptor on hardware but offer zero service layer.
5. **Hospital / home-healthcare operators (Portea, ViMUT Life Link, Emoha, Manzil-equivalents)** in India, Thailand, Singapore provide episodic clinical visits, not 24/7 wearable monitoring. Complementary, not direct competition — and the clearest whitelabel partner.

This dossier covers 28 profiles organised into three tiers. **The single most important strategic finding:** the APAC subscription PERS market is fragmented by country, dominated by local non-profit/government incumbents and security-company bundles, and underserved by global trained-care-specialist subscription brands with transparent pricing, modern caregiver dashboards, and B2B SaaS wholesale channels. **MyLifeLine's winning wedge is the B2B SaaS wholesale model (R39–R180 wholesale per device/mo) sold into retirement-village chains, insurance-company partnerships, home-care agencies, hospital networks, and government tenders, plus a transparent D2C pendant-plus-caregiver-app bundle for the adult-child buyer channel in markets where no equivalent exists (Malaysia, Vietnam, Thailand).**

## Cross-cutting competitive patterns

Reading the 28 profiles, five patterns recur across the APAC PERS landscape:

1. **"Local-owned" + "local-monitored" is the single highest-trust claim in ANZ and Singapore.** MePACS, INS CareCall, LiveLife, CareAlert (AU); St John NZ; SG Assist, BOP, Singapore Red Cross, iWOW, Ready Digital (SG). Foreign-owned brands (VitalCALL/Chubb NYSE: CB, Tunstall PE-owned C Capital, ADT NYSE: ADT) are counter-positioned by these incumbents. **Implication for MyLifeLine:** position as **South-African-built, pan-APAC-distributed** to defuse the foreign-owned narrative; lean into ANZ/SG trust by partnering with locally-owned ARCs (e.g. INS CareCall).

2. **Transparent online pricing is rare.** CareAlert (A$35–A$42/mo), Tunstall (A$31.30/mo), ADT Health (A$29.99–A$41.99/mo), LiveLife (A$567 + A$90/yr SIM), SG Assist CareBell (SGD $20/mo), BOP (SGD $20/mo), ALSOK (JPY 1,870/mo), SECOM (JPY 5,100/mo), ViMUT Life Link (1,000 THB/mo Silver) all publish online. Most Tier-2 startups (DrRing, Portea, Emoha, Preventech, NaviCare, Ready Digital, CareLink escort) and most government/non-profit programmes (HoME+, Korea 119, RDNS CareLink) are quote-only or means-tested. **Implication:** publish full transparent pricing in every APAC currency (AUD/NZD/SGD/JPY/INR/CNY/MYR/THB/VND/HKD/KRW) to capture the digital-native caregiver cohort.

3. **Caregiver-app market is bifurcating.** MePACS, VitalCALL, CareAlert, LiveLife, ALSOK, ADT Health — **no first-party caregiver app** at all. Tunstall "MyHome", INS CareCall Carer App, SG Assist CareGiverCircle, SCHSA Guardian Card family app, DrRing WhatsApp, CareLink dashboard, Emoha app — **basic app**. BOP App (multi-home/multi-user/family-sharing), HOMEGUARD iHere app, Red Cross HoME+ app, Korea 119 caseworker app, Preventech implicit layer — **intermediate**. No Tier-1 brand offers **a modern dashboard with vitals trends + alert log + medication reminders + location history + battery alerts + multi-caregiver family-circle + geofencing + voice-call recording.** **Implication:** a richer caregiver dashboard is the clearest next moat in APAC.

4. **Hospital/government/security-company plays dominate scale but not D2C transparency.** Hospital (ViMUT, Emoha), government (Korea 119, Singapore HDB AAS, SCHSA, St John NZ, RDNS CareLink), and security-company (SECOM, ALSOK, ADT-Chubb, VitalCALL) plays control most of the APAC PERS subscriber base by anchoring on institutional trust. None has a transparent D2C pendant-plus-caregiver-app subscription with caregiver dashboard depth. **Implication:** white-label partnerships with hospital networks (Portea, ViMUT, Emoha, Manzil-equivalents) and security-company home-security cross-sell (SECOM, ALSOK new-build housing; ADT ANZ home-security base) are the B2B distribution wedge.

5. **Mobile GPS pendant with built-in fall detection is the uncontested product wedge.** MePACS Mobile A$385 + A$55/mo (fall detection add-on), LiveLife A$567 + A$90/yr SIM (fall detection built-in), SG Assist CareBell (no fall detection), BOP (no fall detection), ViMUT Help Trigger (fall detection built in), ALSOK Mimamori (no fall detection in core pendant), Korea 119 (no mobile pendant), HoME+ (no fall detection by design), SCHSA PE Link (AI fall-detection pilot only). **Implication:** MyLifeLine EV07BA with built-in fall detection, 4G, own SIM, and SMS-to-family at no monthly fee is the product wedge in markets where the subscription incumbent charges A$42–A$108/mo or doesn't offer mobile at all (Korea 119, HoME+, SCHSA standard PE Link, SECOM My Doctor is home-only).

6. **The ASEAN subscription PERS market is wide open.** Outside Singapore (CareBell, BOP, iWOW, HoME+, Ready Digital) and Malaysia (Preventech, iElder, CorriCare, Penang Care), Thailand (ViMUT, CareLink), Vietnam (NaviCare), and the rest of ASEAN have no major subscription-PERS brand. The MyLifeLine wholesale model can be the **first mover** in Indonesia, Philippines, Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar by partnering with home-care agencies and insurance companies — none of the 28 profiles cover these markets.

## Gap analysis: where MyLifeLine wins against the 28 APAC competitors

Synthesised from each profile's "MyLifeLine opportunity" section:

1. **Caregiver dashboard depth is the #1 cross-cutting gap.** 18 of 28 profiles explicitly cite no first-party caregiver app or shallow app. Modern dashboard (vitals trends, alert log, medication reminders, location history, low-battery alerts, multi-caregiver family-circle, geofencing) is the upgrade path vs MePACS, VitalCALL, CareAlert, ALSOK, ADT Health, Tunstall.

### 6. Avoid the mis-steps from this dossier

- **Don't treat ASLI as a single competitor** — ASLI (Association of Senior Living India) is a non-profit industry body; Emoha Elder Care and SeniorWorld (JUVO) are ASLI-member PERS / eldercare operators.
- **Don't fight Korea 119 Ansim Call or SCHSA 一線通 head-on** — they are free / heavily subsidised government programmes. Integrate, partner, or complement; never compete on price.
- **Don't try to out-bundle SECOM/ALSOK 5-year home-security** — they have 1.4m+ and 530k+ home-security customers respectively. Be the **second pendant** or the **caregiver-dashboard upgrade** for their existing subscribers.
- **Don't underestimate Apple Watch / Xiaomi Mi SOS / Samsung Galaxy Watch fall detection** — 100m+ installed base is real. Build the **post-alert triage layer**, not a competing pendant.
- **Don't promise in-home-only coverage in markets with active outdoor senior populations (JP, KR, HK, MY)** — pendant-only or home-only players (Korea 119, HoME+, SECOM My Doctor home-only) leave a clear mobile gap.
