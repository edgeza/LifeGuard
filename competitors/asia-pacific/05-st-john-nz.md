## Product lineup

- **St John In-Home Alarm** — MSD-listed weekly rental; A$16.85/wk (single alarm); A$7.75/wk additional alarm same address (verified via MSD table 2026).
- **St John Anywhere Alarm** — mobile/cellular alarm with GPS; A$24.95/wk; A$16.85/wk additional alarm same address (MSD table 2026).
- **Free trial period** — typically 1 month "No Charge Trial" for new clients.
- **Rental model only** — St John does not sell alarms outright; rentals keep equipment maintained.
- **Funding pathway** — Disability Allowance via WINZ; ACC cover for accident-related cases.

## Differentiation

St John is the only MSD-accredited PERS in NZ owned by the country's largest ambulance service. Selling point: ambulance integration (press button → 111 dispatch), MSD-eligibility subsidy pathway, free trial, rental-only model that keeps hardware maintained, and brand-trust (St John brand is the closest NZ analog to the Red Cross brand).

## Strengths

- ✅ Ambulance integration — unique in NZ; button press can flow directly to St John ambulance service.
- ✅ MSD-eligibility for Disability Allowance — government subsidised for low-income elderly.
- ✅ Rental-only model — hardware stays maintained and upgraded at no extra cost.
- ✅ Free in-home demonstration + free trial month.
- ✅ New Zealand-wide; 24/7 NZ monitoring.
- ✅ Strongest trust brand in NZ (St John badge recognised >95% of NZ population).

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ Weekly rental pricing is high (~NZ$73/mo in-home; ~NZ$108/mo Anywhere) vs Australian benchmarks (A$31–A$45/mo) and international no-monthly-fee rivals.
- ❌ No published first-party caregiver app.
- ❌ No device purchase option (rental-only) — customers who want ownership cannot buy out.
- ❌ Falls detection add-on only; not built into the entry SKU.
- ❌ Foreign-software dependency — St John NZ uses monitoring software that competes with global ARC platforms (Verklizan UMO etc.) and lacks depth.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **No-monthly-fee MyLifeLine × EV07BA at NZD pricing.** A NZD-sold pendant that calls up to 6 family and includes 111 as one contact (LiveLife AU/NZ model) undercuts St John on monthly cost and gives families digital visibility.
- **Caregiver app for adult-child buyers.** St John has none; a caregiver dashboard would resonate with the Gen-X adult-child demographic.
- **Weekly vs monthly pricing transparency.** A monthly published price ladder is more comparable to AU/UK D2C buyers.
- **B2G partnership for WINZ-funded cohort.** St John is the incumbent; a parallel MyLifeLine-funded-channel that doesn't require WINZ pre-clearance could be a switcher path.
