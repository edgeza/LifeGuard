## Differentiation

Singlewire InformaCast is the **facility-based MNS incumbent for K-12 and higher-ed**: a 20-year platform with five panic-button modalities (badge / app / keyboard / desk phone / mounted) and tight visitor-management integration (Visitor Aware). InformaCast Alert Badge is a direct Alyssa's-Law competitor to Motorola's Rave Panic Button.

## Strengths

- ✅ Five panic-button modalities including the InformaCast Wearable Alert Badge — floor- and room-level location accuracy.
- ✅ Alyssa's-Law-compliant silent alerting that reaches local dispatchers.
- ✅ Tight integration with student-information systems (SIS), LMS, and visitor-management — K-12 native.
- ✅ 1B+ devices notified / 1.3M+ users — operational scale signal.
- ✅ 99.99% system uptime SLA — strong enterprise IT positioning.
- ✅ Independent company post-2024 (no parent-company channel conflict).

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ No risk-intelligence layer (no Dataminr-style threat feed, no travel-risk management).
- ❌ No IPAWS / WEA public-warning franchise at federal level.
- ❌ K-12 / higher-ed skew — limited Fortune 500 corporate CEM RFPs.
- ❌ Hardware-product dominance means longer deployment cycles vs. SaaS-only challengers.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Wearable Alert Badge for individuals is the missing consumer twin.** Singlewire sells panic-button badges to school districts; a personal safety pendant with the same Alyssa's-Law-grade reliability, but routing to 911 + caregiver app + family emergency contacts, is the right-shaped MyLifeLine product.
- **Visitor Aware for senior-living facilities is a partnership wedge.** Singlewire's K-12 visitor-management product could be adapted for assisted-living / memory-care facilities — a B2B channel that pairs with MyLifeLine's personal-safety product for the resident.
- **No consumer-grade personal alerting subscription exists.** Singlewire's smallest SKU is a school district; the entire consumer tier is unoccupied.
