## Differentiation

Regroup is the mid-market alternative to Everbridge — published pricing opacity is similar but customer-satisfaction reputation (Regroup 2.0 launched 2023) is a step above Everbridge for smaller organisations that don't need Everbridge's enterprise scale. Free COVID-era adoption in education built an installed base that migrated to paid contracts.

## Strengths

- ✅ IPAWS-capable since 2015 — public-warning certified for state/local govt and university emergency-management RFPs.
- ✅ 4,000+ customers including state/local govt (Adams County CO, City of Huntsville) — credible mid-market public-sector footprint.
- ✅ Threat Intelligence Suite added 2023 — keeps pace with AlertMedia / Everbridge without analyst team.
- ✅ 1B+ messages/year — operational scale signal.
- ✅ Regroup 2.0 (2023) refresh — feature parity with category leaders on incident management.
- ✅ Unlimited 24/7 customer support from real humans — positioning copy point.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ Limited international footprint vs. Everbridge (6,700+ / 87 countries) and AlertMedia (150+ countries).
- ❌ No first-party panic-button hardware (vs. Rave, Singlewire, Alertus).
- ❌ Smaller brand — fewer Gartner/Forrester placements; less analyst coverage.
- ❌ No risk-intelligence depth comparable to Dataminr / AlertMedia.
- ❌ Mid-market positioning means Fortune 500 RFPs are scarce.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Mid-market CEM doesn't have a personal-safety peer.** Regroup's customer is a county, college, or city — no consumer-tier. A "personal-fee" tier modelled on Regroup's pricing transparency would be a wedge.
- **Public-warning-to-consumer bridge.** Regroup's IPAWS certification is a backend capability; an individual could subscribe to IPAWS-style alerts for their specific address, but no consumer product exists today.
- **Threat Intelligence Suite for individuals is unbuilt.** Severe-weather + civil-unrest + active-threat risk feed for $5–$10/mo is the right-shaped MyLifeLine product.
