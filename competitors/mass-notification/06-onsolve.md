## Differentiation

OnSolve — merged with GardaWorld's Crisis24 in 2024 — is now the **integrated risk-management-plus-mass-notification challenger** to Everbridge and AlertMedia. The combination layers Crisis24's 24/7 Global Operations Centers, human risk analysts, and AI risk intelligence (Horizon) on top of OnSolve's mid-market mass-notification SaaS. This is the explicit answer to AlertMedia's "analyst-supervised AI" positioning.

## Strengths

- ✅ Combined GardaWorld + Crisis24 footprint — 6 continents, 24/7 Global Operations Centers, ~400 OnSolve employees.
- ✅ Crisis24 Horizon + TopoONE — AI + human analyst risk-intelligence stack that competes with Dataminr in physical-security space.
- ✅ IPAWS-capable public-warning (inherited from OnSolve legacy).
- ✅ Multi-billion-alerts-per-year scale signal.
- ✅ Travel risk + global security assistance + medical evacuation bundles — unique among CEM-only platforms.
- ✅ GardaWorld balance sheet — Canadian private; financial firepower for continued investment.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ Multiple brand consolidation waves (2017 OnSolve rebrand, 2024 Crisis24 merger) — enterprise customer-base confusion.
- ❌ No first-party panic-button hardware (vs. Rave/Singlewire/Alertus).
- ❌ Gartner / Forrester Wave placements lag Everbridge and AlertMedia in published 2025–2026 reports.
- ❌ Canadian private ownership means less public financial transparency than EVBG.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Travel-risk subscription for individuals is white space.** Crisis24 sells travel-risk packages to corporate security teams at six-figure ACVs; the same data layered into a personal-travel subscription ($10–$30/mo) is the right-shaped consumer product.
- **Risk-intelligence for caregivers.** Crisis24's Horizon feed covers severe weather, civil unrest, terrorism, kidnapping — exactly what a family member wants to see about a loved one's travel or location.
- **No incumbent consumer CEM.** The GardaWorld portfolio is enterprise-only; nobody bundles "OnSolve + Crisis24 for a family." MyLifeLine could brand this as the "personal duty-of-care" subscription.
