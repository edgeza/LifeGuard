## Differentiation

Resgrid is the **only open-source, AI-native CAD / mass-notification platform** with natural-language dispatch (SMS / WhatsApp / Discord / Slack / Telegram / Teams / web chat). Its free tier and "no contracts, no sales calls" model is the radical anti-Everbridge in the CEM space — anyone can sign up and start dispatching.

## Strengths

- ✅ Open-source core on GitHub (11 repos); free tier; self-hostable — radically different from Everbridge's enterprise-only model.
- ✅ AI-native natural-language dispatch ("I'm responding to the structure fire on Main St" → status automatically set).
- ✅ 14+ modules (dispatching, messaging, shifts, calendar, tracking, reporting) in a single platform.
- ✅ Multi-channel messaging (SMS, voice, email, push, WhatsApp, Discord, Slack, Telegram, Teams, web chat) — broader than CEM-internal collaboration tools.
- ✅ 5 dedicated applications (Personnel, Units, Dispatchers, Stations, Public) covering the full dispatch ecosystem.
- ✅ No contracts, no implementation fees — self-service onboarding in minutes.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ No risk-intelligence layer (no Dataminr-style threat feed).
- ❌ No IPAWS / WEA federal layer.
- ❌ Smaller brand — limited analyst coverage; volunteer / SMB skew.
- ❌ No panic-button hardware.
- ❌ No AI-powered analytics like AlertMedia Analytics 2026.
- ❌ Total addressable market skews volunteer fire / EMS / small industrial dispatch — not Fortune 500 enterprise.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Self-service / no-contract pricing is the right consumer shape.** Resgrid proves that mass-notification infrastructure can be sold without sales calls and at low price points — exactly MyLifeLine's pitch.
- **Natural-language dispatch UX for panic-button.** "Help me, I've fallen" → automated location + 911 routing + caregiver notification is the consumer version of Resgrid's CAD UX.
- **Open-source credibility.** MyLifeLine can reference Resgrid's open-source model in copy ("we publish our safety protocol openly") without competing on architecture.
