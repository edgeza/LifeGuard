## Differentiation

Veoci is the **no-code virtual EOC** — its differentiator is the low-code/no-code workflow builder that lets emergency managers configure their own boards, forms, and dashboards without IT tickets. Where Everbridge and AlertMedia sell finished products, Veoci is a platform for agencies to build their own. That makes it sticky for sophisticated buyers but harder for smaller organisations.

## Strengths

- ✅ Low-code/no-code workflow builder — emergency managers can customise without IT.
- ✅ 60+ airports — aviation-segment depth that Everbridge / AlertMedia cannot match.
- ✅ Higher-ed reference list (Syracuse, U Alabama) — credibility in university emergency management.
- ✅ Veoci Mass Notification is bundled into the EOC platform — single-vendor emergency-management stack.
- ✅ Healthcare, government, finance, utilities verticals — broader than K-12-only competitors.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ Smaller brand than Everbridge / AlertMedia — limited analyst coverage.
- ❌ No first-party risk-intelligence feed (vs. Dataminr / AlertMedia).
- ❌ No panic-button hardware (vs. Rave / Singlewire / Alertus).
- ❌ No IPAWS / WEA federal layer.
- ❌ Pricing opacity is consistent with mid-market CEM (no public list price).

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **No-code EOC is the wrong shape for consumer.** Veoci's strength is configuration; a personal safety app should be turn-key with no configuration. Differentiation opportunity.
- **Aviation-grade travel-risk intel for individuals.** Veoci serves 60+ airports; a personal subscription that wraps an individual's travel itinerary through the same airport emergency-management layer is the consumer wedge.
- **No consumer-grade Veoci product exists.** Veoci's smallest customer is a city or airport; the consumer tier is unoccupied.
