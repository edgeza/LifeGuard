## Differentiation

CrisisGo is the **K-12-pure-play** in the mass-notification market — 16,000+ schools is the largest K-12-specific MNS installed base. CrisisGo Direct911 routes a panic-button press directly to 911 dispatchers with school profile data, fulfilling Alyssa's-Law requirements while staying in a SaaS-only model (no proprietary hardware).

## Strengths

- ✅ 16,000+ schools — largest K-12-specific mass-notification installed base.
- ✅ $999/year entry pricing — the most transparent K-12 MNS pricing in the market.
- ✅ CrisisGo Direct911 — direct-to-911 routing with school profile data (no proprietary panic-button hardware required).
- ✅ Alyssa's-Law-compliant silent alerting with persona-based options.
- ✅ FirstNet App Catalog listed — public-safety-grade credibility.
- ✅ Geo-redundant platform on AWS with multi-site + multi-organisation hierarchy included.
- ✅ 48M+ messages/year — operational scale in K-12 segment.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ K-12-only focus — no enterprise, federal, healthcare, manufacturing presence.
- ❌ No first-party panic-button hardware (vs. Rave Badge, Singlewire Alert Badge).
- ❌ No risk-intelligence layer (no Dataminr-style threat feed).
- ❌ Smaller brand than Everbridge/AlertMedia/OnSolve — limited inbound demand-gen.
- ❌ Reviews cite "no real-time building attendance information" as a gap ⚠️.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **The K-12 model is the prototype for senior / lone-worker alerting.** CrisisGo's "everyone acknowledges → first responder gets profile data → family gets notification" is the right architecture for personal safety; MyLifeLine can borrow the workflow.
- **Direct911 is the consumer unlock.** CrisisGo Direct911 routes panic presses to 911 — a personal MyLifeLine pendant doing the same for at-home individuals is a defensible product narrative.
- **$999/year entry pricing proves the SaaS model can be transparent.** MyLifeLine's consumer pricing should reference K-12 MNS benchmarks as proof of low-cost SaaS viability.
