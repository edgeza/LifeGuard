# MySOS

> **Category:** Standalone voice-enabled panic button + corporate estate deployment
> **Website:** https://mysos.co.za

## Overview
MySOS sells a **standalone voice-enabled panic button** that works over GSM (no smartphone required) — the closest direct competitor to MyLifeLine's wearable pendant line on the hardware axis. The device has built-in two-way voice, GPS, and connects to a 24/7 response team with access to 2,000+ armed-response vehicles and national paramedic networks. Also deployed in estates and retirement villages as a corporate offering.

## Core Offering
- **MySOS Panic Button** — small, GSM-connected, two-way voice. Senior-friendly wearable.
- **Direct voice-to-response-team** without a phone.
- **Corporate / Estates / Retirement Villages:** voice-enabled pendant extends protection beyond the estate gate; caregivers and responders can talk to the resident directly through the device.
- Armed-response, medical, roadside assistance all routed from the same button.

## Pricing
Not publicly listed; subscription-based with corporate volume pricing.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Voice-enabled pendant** is a head-on hardware competitor — same form factor and same "no-phone-needed" pitch as MyLifeLine's EV04/EV07.
- Strong retirement-village pitch (same vertical MyLifeLine targets).
- "Speak to your emergency responder directly" via the device is a clearer UX than MyLifeLine's "control room calls you back".
- South-African market heritage.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- No fall detection, no HR monitor, no step counter, no smartwatch variant like MyLifeLine EV06.
- No B2B SaaS platform / API play — MyLifeLine licenses its cloud platform; MySOS appears to be a closed product/service.
- Lacks the medical/allergy operator workflow documented in MyLifeLine's manual.
- Smaller partner ecosystem on the public-side (no bank/insurance white-label deals).

## Target Customer
Retirement villages, security estates, individual seniors, families wanting a no-phone pendant.
