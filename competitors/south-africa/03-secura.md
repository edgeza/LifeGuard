# Secura

> **Category:** Consumer panic button app + hardware bundle
> **Website:** https://www.secura.co.za

## Overview
Secura is a smartphone-based personal safety app that connects the user to "South Africa's largest network of private armed response" — backed by 24/7 control-room support and an optional physical panic button. Marketed as "armed response in your pocket".

## Core Offering
- **In-app panic button** — single-tap SOS, control room calls back, dispatches nearest armed responder.
- **Physical panic button** sold as standalone or bundled with subscription.
- **Three response tiers:** armed response, medical, roadside/security standby.
- **Family / multi-user** plans available.
- Coverage claim: "entire country" with SAPS contacted where private coverage is absent.

## Pricing
- Standalone panic button: **R899** once-off.
- Panic button + 6-month subscription: **R1,299**.
- Panic button + 12-month subscription: **R1,399**.
- Subscription only: ~R49/month (per marketing).

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- Strong reviews (4.6 ★ on App Store) with testimonials about sub-60-second call-back.
- Hardware + subscription bundle is a clear, simple SKU strategy; MyLifeLine sells wearables separately from platform fees.
- Single price-point decision for the customer ("pay once + small monthly") rather than the device-plus-platform-plus-SMS-cost breakdown MyLifeLine exposes.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- Hardware is a basic button — no fall detection, no HR, no screen, no two-way voice like MyLifeLine's EV06.
- App-first; the pendant is a phone-dependent accessory, not a fully standalone device with its own SIM like MyLifeLine's EV04/EV07.
- No elderly/medical positioning (no medical/allergy profile, no chronic-condition workflows).
- Lacks the B2B control-room platform play MyLifeLine pitches to security companies and estates.

## Target Customer
Individual consumers and families wanting the cheapest one-app-does-it-all safety subscription.
