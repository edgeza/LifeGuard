# eSecure

> **Category:** Consumer panic app with optional smartwatch
> **Website:** https://www.esecure.co.za

## Overview
eSecure is a smartphone-based rapid-response panic button that aggregates 500+ armed-response companies, SAPS, and government/private ambulance services on a single platform. Notable for being one of the earlier apps to support a **smartwatch panic activation** and an **Android home-screen widget**, so the user doesn't have to open the app.

## Core Offering
- **Single-tap SOS** in app or via smartwatch / Android widget.
- **Real-time dispatch** to nearest armed-response, SAPS, or private ambulance.
- **6,000+ end users**, 500+ armed-response partners nationwide.
- Quotes 15-minute help arrival depending on location/internet coverage.

## Pricing
Not publicly listed; subscription-based (sales-led).

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- Smartwatch integration (early SA mover on this) lets elderly/frail users trigger from the wrist without a phone.
- Android widget for one-tap home-screen activation is a usability win — MyLifeLine doesn't ship a widget.
- Aggregator model (lots of partners) gives wide national coverage.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- No proprietary pendant line; relies on third-party smartwatches.
- "15 minutes" is a weak response SLA — MyLifeLine's control-room workflow targets sub-minute operator pickup with two-way voice.
- Lacks the elderly/medical workflow (no fall detection on a dedicated pendant, no medical/allergy profile surfaced to operators).
- No B2B platform — only direct consumer subscription.

## Target Customer
Consumers wanting a quick-install panic app; smartwatch owners; elderly with caregiver-provided smartwatches.
