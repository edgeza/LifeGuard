# AURA

> **Category:** Response-network platform (B2B + white-label consumer apps)
> **Headquarters:** Johannesburg, South Africa (operates in ZA, UK, KE, US)
> **Website:** https://www.aurasos.com

## Overview
AURA is South Africa's largest smart-security and emergency-response dispatch network. It is the platform-of-record behind many of the consumer panic apps that appear on Google Play under the "Aura-App" developer account, including **GuardMe 2.0**, **Tracker CareGuard**, **Copwatch**, **Bidvest Panic Alert**, **PPA (Presence Panic App)**, **Always Safe** and others. It exposes white-label apps, REST APIs, and IoT device integrations for any partner (banks, insurers, telcos, security companies) that wants to offer "press-a-button-and-help-arrives" without building a control room.

## Core Offering
- **On-demand panic dispatch:** nearest vetted armed-response, medical or roadside-assistance responder autonomously routed to a GPS location.
- **One-tap SOS** in mobile app, plus integration with vehicle telematics and lone-worker devices.
- **24/7 control room** in Johannesburg verifying emergencies, calling the user, dispatching, and following through to resolution.
- **Coverage:** "Core" SA, UK, Kenya, major US cities. Global 911 routing everywhere else.
- **Tiered service:** medical-only, security-only, roadside, or combined.

## Pricing
Not directly published; pricing is set by each white-label partner (e.g. GuardMe R19.90/m, Tracker CareGuard from R49/m).

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- Massive network effect (250+ partner response companies, 5,000+ responders, 2,500+ vehicles).
- White-label / API play — AURA is infrastructure MyLifeLine does not offer, which is a credible partnership-or-competitor threat.
- Embeds in tier-1 brands (FNB, RMB, Tracker, Bidvest, Discovery) → distribution MyLifeLine cannot match.
- Telematics / vehicle / lone-worker verticals in addition to personal panic.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- AURA is **not wearable-first**; its DNA is the dispatch network and the smartphone app. Wearable devices are a partner integration (Discovery Insure, Tracker, etc.) — not its own curated pendant/watch lineup.
- No proprietary medical-grade pendant with fall detection and HR monitoring like MyLifeLine's EV06/EV07 range.
- Customer relationship is owned by the white-label partner (FNB, Discovery, Tracker…), so AURA itself has weak end-consumer brand.

## Target Customer
Banks, insurers, security companies, telcos, fleet operators, lone-worker deployments, embedded vehicle telematics.
