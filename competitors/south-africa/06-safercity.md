# SaferCity

> **Category:** Consumer panic app + covert wearable; B2B panic-as-a-service
> **Website:** https://safercity.co.za

## Overview
SaferCity markets itself as "Africa's most trusted personal safety network," one-tap app connected to 522 Community Policing Forums and 4,000+ first responders across all 9 provinces. Also sells a discrete 4G GPS panic button wearable and runs an enterprise B2B panic platform for multinationals.

## Core Offering
- **One-tap SOS** sends GPS to armed response, SAPS and family; <5-minute average national response.
- **4G GPS Panic Button Express** (R-priced device, IP67, two-way voice, geo-fencing, ±2.5 m GPS, 1-3 day battery, magnetic USB charging). Works standalone or paired with the app.
- **Covert GPS wearables** with silent panic, biometric verification, rescue protocol integration (anti-kidnapping positioning).
- **B2B white-label deployments** for multinationals with 5,000+ staff — local response partners, local compliance, global standards.

## Pricing
- Subscription tiers Basic / Premium (not publicly listed).
- Hardware Panic Button Express sold separately (device-only available to existing subscribers).

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **B2B enterprise play** with white-label, local-compliance, multinational-rollout capability — directly competitive with MyLifeLine's security-company / estate / retirement-village pitch.
- Covert anti-kidnapping wearable is a niche MyLifeLine does not address.
- Strong CPF integration (522 forums) for community-level response.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- Pendant is generic OEM hardware (likely Chinese-sourced) — not the curated medical-grade lineup MyLifeLine offers (EV04/06/07/12 with HR, fall, step counter).
- No clear elderly/medical positioning (no HR monitor, no SpO₂, no chronic-condition workflows).
- "Covert anti-kidnapping" is corporate/exec niche — doesn't help MyLifeLine's retirement-village / frail-care vertical.
- 4,000+ "first responders" claim is loose (mix of CPFs, security companies, medics).

## Target Customer
Urban consumers, executives, multinationals seeking enterprise panic platform, community-policing adopters.
