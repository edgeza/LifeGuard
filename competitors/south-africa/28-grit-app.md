# GRIT App (Gender Rights in Tech)

> **Category:** GBV-focused survivor safety app (free, data-free)
> **Website:** https://www.gritapp.org.za (App Store: id1616602924)

## Overview
GRIT is a free, data-free survivor-centred safety + legal-tech app combining immediate emergency tools with trauma-informed legal support and SRHR guidance. Built for SA's GBV crisis. Features a 5-second panic activation with armed-response dispatch and audio evidence vault.

## Core Offering
- **Panic Button** — 5-second hold → armed response dispatched in <8 min; auto-records 20-sec audio → Evidence Vault.
- **Real-time GPS tracking** during panic.
- **Evidence Vault** — encrypted storage for photos, video, audio, time-stamped case documentation.
- **Find Support Near Me** — geo-located shelters, counselling, legal aid, healthcare, police.
- **Zuzi AI chatbot** — trauma-informed, multilingual SRHR / legal / mental-health guidance.
- **Report Misuse of Power** — human-rights violation reporting.
- **100% free, data-free.**

## Pricing
Free.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **100% free, data-free** — strongest price disruption in market.
- 20-sec audio evidence on panic = legal utility MyLifeLine lacks.
- Trauma-informed design + AI chatbot is unique.
- Donor-funded so no revenue pressure on user.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- **GBV survivor niche** — narrow audience vs MyLifeLine's broader elderly / family / security-company positioning.
- No wearable pendant / medical alert watch.
- No fall detection / HR / chronic-condition profile.
- No B2B SaaS platform.
- App-only — phone-dependent.

## Target Customer
GBV survivors, vulnerable women, social-sector NGOs, donor-funded programmes.
