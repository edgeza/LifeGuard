# Netstar Companion

> **Category:** Consumer panic app, by Altron's vehicle-tracking brand Netstar
> **Website:** https://www.netstar.co.za/companion

## Overview
Netstar Companion is Altron's standalone personal-safety app, leveraging Netstar's vehicle-tracking / recovery heritage. The app dispatches the nearest of 1,500+ armed responders to the user's GPS, with a distinctive **shake-to-activate** feature and a 20-second audio/video recording triggered on every panic.

## Core Offering
- **Mobile panic button** — autonomous dispatch to 5 closest response vehicles; control room calls user to verify.
- **Shake-to-activate** — phone shake fires the panic without opening the app (useful when phone is in hand/pocket).
- **20-second audio/video evidence recording** automatically uploaded to Netstar servers for legal use.
- **SMS to 5 emergency contacts** with live-location link.
- **Family plans** — additional members at reduced fee.

## Pricing
- Single member: **R69/month**
- Family: **R198/month**

## Pricing comparison
App Store rating is poor (2.6 ★ / 19 reviews), with recurring complaints about OTP delivery on registration.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Evidence recording** (20-sec audio/video) is a unique trust/legal differentiator MyLifeLine doesn't ship.
- **Shake-to-activate** is a covert panic mode — useful under duress.
- Netstar/Altron distribution muscle for fleet/insurance partnerships.
- Cheaper than MyLifeLine's R180/month platform fee (but MyLifeLine R180 is per-device, not per-seat).

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- App-only — no standalone wearable pendant or elderly watch.
- App Store rating 2.6 ★ signals onboarding UX problems.
- No fall detection, no medical-grade wearable.
- No medical/allergy operator workflow.
- No B2B control-room platform (it is a direct-to-consumer app on top of AURA-style responder network).

## Target Customer
Existing Netstar vehicle-tracking customers, individual commuters, families.
