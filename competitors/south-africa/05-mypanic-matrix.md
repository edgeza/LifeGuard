# MyPanic (Matrix)

> **Category:** Consumer panic app, white-labelled by Matrix Vehicle Assurance
> **Website:** https://matrix.co.za/mypanic

## Overview
MyPanic is a smartphone personal-safety app sold by Matrix (a long-established SA insurance / roadside brand). It routes a one-tap SOS into a 24/7 national emergency-response network, dispatching armed-response or medical vehicles to the user's GPS. The app also offers proactive features like "Ride & Meet" timers for solo travel.

## Core Offering
- **Press-and-hold "Help Me!"** button → 24/7 control room is alerted, calls user, dispatches closest armed or medical vehicle.
- **Nationwide coverage** claim across urban, rural, farming and small-town areas.
- **Family plans:** single R49/m, double R69/m, family-of-5 R99/m. Subscriber can extend to dependents at any address.
- **Privacy-first design:** no constant background tracking; location shared only on SOS.

## Pricing
- Single: R49 / month
- Double: R69 / month
- Family of 5: R99 / month

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- Family-of-5 at R99 is exceptional price-aggressiveness — undercuts most consumer panic apps per-seat.
- Matrix insurance parent gives bundling potential with vehicle / household policies.
- Privacy posture is explicit (no background tracking) — a useful trust signal post-POPIA.
- "Ride & Meet" timer is a useful proactive feature MyLifeLine lacks.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- App-only — **no wearable pendant or medical alert watch** in their lineup. Pure smartphone product.
- No fall detection, no two-way voice from a dedicated device, no medical/allergy operator profile.
- Lacks the B2B control-room software play (Matrix is selling to consumers, not licensing platform to other security companies like MyLifeLine does).
- Limited geographic provenance for actual coverage outside metros.

## Target Customer
Individual SA consumers and families wanting cheap one-app panic coverage; Matrix insurance customers.
