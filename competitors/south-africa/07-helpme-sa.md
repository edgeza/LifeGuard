# HelpMe SA

> **Category:** Data-free consumer panic app + lifestyle safety
> **Website:** https://helpmesa.co.za

## Overview
HelpMe is a "data-free" personal-safety app (functions without airtime/data bundles — a SA-first differentiator). It runs a 24/7 Crisis Management Centre with a self-reported 19-second average response time, and aggregates 100+ emergency-service providers and 7,000+ responders nationwide.

## Core Offering
- **Panic button** — armed response, medical, fire, roadside, all from one app.
- **Data-free** operation across major SA networks (key for low-income / load-shedding scenarios).
- **Community Alert** and **Home Alert** modes — phone-as-personal-panic for at-home use.
- **Family features:** child location tracking, "loved-one" battery and location view, child data-free help request.
- **Home-security referrals** — pairs you with a local armed-response contract if you don't have an alarm.

## Pricing
Subscription-based; not publicly listed on website.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Data-free** is a meaningful moat in SA — many users can't or won't consume mobile data for safety. MyLifeLine's app is conventional.
- Lifestyle integration (child tracking, home referrals, fire dispatch) is broader than MyLifeLine's focused elderly/security positioning.
- 19-second claim is competitive with the best in market.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- **No wearable panic pendant** — fully app-dependent.
- No medical-grade fall detection or HR monitoring.
- No B2B platform (HelpMe is consumer-direct; MyLifeLine licenses its cloud platform to security companies and estates).
- "Family" features are GPS-sharing lite, not a real medical-profile platform.

## Target Customer
Mass-market SA consumers (data-conscious), parents, lower-income users, fire / medical / roadside generalists.
