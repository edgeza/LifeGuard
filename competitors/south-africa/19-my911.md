# MY911

> **Category:** Wearable panic pendant + smartwatch (4G, with fall detection)
> **Website:** https://my911.co.za

## Overview
MY911 sells a standalone 4G pendant (ERD PB 3 / PB 4) and an **Apple-Watch-linked smartwatch panic app**. Once activated, the device is fully standalone — no phone required. Targets both **sports enthusiasts** and **elderly** users (fall detection adjustable for sensitivity).

## Core Offering
- **MY911 Pendant (PB 3):** 61 × 44 × 16 mm, 35 g, lanyard/belt-clip/wrist-band, 4G, GPS, fall detection, docking station.
- **MY911 Pendant (PB 4):** 62 × 47 × 17 mm, 53 g, lanyard + belt clip, no wrist band.
- **MY911 Smartwatch app:** pairs with iOS smartwatch so SOS is reachable from wrist without phone.
- **36-month included service** on once-off device purchase; roaming priority.
- Adjustable fall-detection sensitivity (high for elderly, low for sport).
- **Shared device model** — one panic button can be shared across family with a passphrase.

## Pricing
Once-off device purchase (incl. 36 months service). Contact for current pricing.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Roaming priority** on 4G — useful for SA cross-border travellers and regional users.
- **Shared family device** model is creative — one hardware SKU covers multiple family members.
- Adjustable fall sensitivity is genuinely useful across age groups.
- Smartwatch integration widens appeal beyond pendant-only market.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- 36-month service lock-in is restrictive vs MyLifeLine's monthly R180 model.
- No documented HR monitor / SpO₂ — fall detection only.
- No B2B platform play.
- No medical/allergy operator profile workflow.
- Smaller brand / less prominent in SA market than MyLifeLine's 2015 incumbent position.

## Target Customer
Active seniors, sports people, frequent travellers, families wanting shared panic hardware.
