# Namola

> **Category:** Consumer panic / family-safety app
> **Operator:** MultiChoice (DStv parent) — owned by Naspers
> **Website:** https://www.namola.com

## Overview
Namola is one of South Africa's most recognised personal-safety apps (>500,000 Android installs). It started with public-services dispatch (police/ambulance/fire) and has layered a paid private-response tier on top. Recent repositioning adds family location ("Places" with geofenced arrival/departure alerts) and verified neighbourhood-watch incident reporting.

## Core Offering
- **SOS button** — 2-second hold, silent mode, no menus. Real operator calls back in <30 s.
- **Public panic (Essential, free):** dispatches 112 / SAPS / ambulance to GPS coordinates.
- **Private Plus (R59/m):** dispatch into AURA's private armed-response and medical network, plus post-incident trauma callback.
- **Places & smart alerts:** geofenced arrival/departure notifications for family members.
- **Communities / Neighbourhood Watch** with verified reports and incident map.

## Pricing
- **Free / Essential:** R0 (public dispatch + 2 contacts + travel history).
- **Plus:** from R59/m (DStv subscribers get 50% discount). Whole-family pricing, no per-property contract.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- Strong consumer brand; widely advertised on DStv.
- Tiered model (free → paid) gets a user into the funnel and upgrades them later.
- MultiChoice backing = financial muscle and customer base.
- Family-circles feature with travel history is more mature than MyLifeLine's emergency-contact SMS model.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- App-first; no proprietary wearable pendant or medical alert watch.
- No fall detection on a dedicated elderly device — falls are addressed via phone shake/accelerometer, not a medical-grade wearable.
- Public dispatch is unreliable outside metros (Namola admits 112 is "notoriously unreliable") — it's basically a UX wrapper around the same failing state service.
- No medical/allergy profile workflow in the operator console (MyLifeLine's operator sees medical info; Namola operators largely route to responders).

## Target Customer
General public, families, DStv subscribers, urban residents.
