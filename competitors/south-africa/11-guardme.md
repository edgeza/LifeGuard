# GuardMe 2.0

> **Category:** Bank-distributed consumer panic app (FNB / RMB Private Bank)
> **Website:** https://www.ebucks.com/web/eBucks/partners/guardme.jsp

## Overview
GuardMe is an FNB/RMB Private Bank-distributed panic app built on the AURA dispatch platform. Distributed via the FNB and RMB mobile apps (so 4M+ FNB customers have one-tap access to it), with a standalone GuardMe 2.0 app for non-FNB members. Bundles into the eBucks rewards programme — first 3 months free, R19.90/m thereafter, up to 100% back in eBucks.

## Core Offering
- **One-tap "Emergency help"** in FNB/RMB app or standalone GuardMe 2.0.
- **250 armed-response companies, 2,500 vehicles, 5,000 personnel** via AURA.
- Live location, control-room callback, automatic dispatch if user can't answer.
- 6-member max household, eBucks earn cap.

## Pricing
- **First 3 months free** for qualifying FNB/RMB customers.
- **R19.90/member/month** thereafter.
- Up to 100% back in eBucks depending on tier (cap 6 members).
- Available to non-FNB subscribers via standalone app at same R19.90 rate.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Distribution moat:** sits inside the FNB app that millions of South Africans already use — a frictionless acquisition channel MyLifeLine cannot match.
- Lowest effective price in market (effectively R0 with eBucks earn-back).
- AURA platform backing = good response coverage.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- App-only (no pendant, no medical watch, no elderly wearable).
- No fall detection / HR / medical profile — the elderly angle is not addressed.
- Bank-channel lock-in: outside FNB/RMB ecosystem, brand has little pull.
- No B2B platform play — it's a consumer white-label on AURA.

## Target Customer
FNB / RMB Private Bank customers (massive SA banking footprint); families wanting cheapest reliable panic app.
