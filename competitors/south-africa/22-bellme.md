# BellMe

> **Category:** In-home wireless nurse-call / elderly panic button (RF-based)
> **Website:** https://bellme.co.za/panic-button-for-the-elderly/

## Overview
BellMe sells a **radio-frequency-based in-home panic button system** — not cellular, not Wi-Fi, not app-based. A wearable call button (lanyard, waterproof) talks via 433.92 MHz RF to a plug-in LED receiver that beeps/flashes in the caregiver's room. Targets frail-care facilities, retirement estates, and home-based elderly care.

## Core Offering
- **Panic call button** — 6 cm, 40 g, 100% waterproof, A23 battery (1-2 year life), soft-press, lanyard or mountable.
- **LED Light Receiver** — plugs into wall, loud continuous beep + red flash until cancel pressed.
- **RF range** 100-150 m (extendable to 300 m with repeaters).
- Stores up to 99 buttons on one receiver — ideal for retirement villages.
- **No monthly fees, no internet required.**

## Pricing
- System once-off purchase.
- Delivery R110 ex VAT nationwide.
- 5-year warranty on call buttons, 2-year on receiver.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Zero ongoing subscription** — radically cheaper TCO for retirement estates (vs MyLifeLine R180/m/device).
- **No network dependency** — works during load-shedding, no SIM, no signal issues.
- Battery lasts 1-2 years (vs daily charging on MyLifeLine wearables).
- Ideal for **in-facility** deployment (nursing homes, frail-care) where the user is in a known location, not out and about.
- 99-button pairing = scales to a village of 99 residents on one receiver.

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- **In-home only** — no GPS, no out-of-home coverage. MyLifeLine is mobile-anywhere.
- No control-room dispatch — the alert goes to whoever is in the building with the LED receiver.
- No fall detection, no HR, no two-way voice.
- Not a personal emergency response service (PERS) — it's a nurse-call.
- No smartphone / family SMS alerts.

## Target Customer
Frail-care facilities, retirement villages (in-room alerting), home-based elderly care with an on-site caregiver.
