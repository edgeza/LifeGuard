## Differentiation

The strongest current panic-jewellery benchmark: a genuinely discreet fashion accessory tied to ADT professional response, rather than only notifying friends. Its core limitation is architectural—the charm is a Bluetooth remote for the phone, not a standalone tracker.

## Strengths

- ✅ Jewellery-first design lowers medical-alert stigma.
- ✅ ADT agents, optional emergency dispatch, live video/chat and safety timers create a richer response layer than peer-to-peer buttons.
- ✅ Multi-year battery removes daily charging friction.
- ✅ Current official pricing and app support are publicly visible.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ Cannot alert if the phone is absent, uncharged, out of Bluetooth range, or lacks data service.
- ❌ No automatic fall detection.
- ❌ Ongoing subscription is required for emergency-service access; without a plan the hardware has little safety value.
- ⚠️ Published starting and typical subscription prices differ because promotions change.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- Build jewellery aesthetics around a standalone LTE/GNSS module or clearly communicate phone dependency.
- Offer fall detection and caregiver health context without making the device look clinical.
- Compete on transparent, stable all-in pricing and Canada-specific dispatch coverage.
