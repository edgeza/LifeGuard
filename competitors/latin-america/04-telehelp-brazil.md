## Product lineup

- **Proteção Dentro de Casa** — in-home system: 4G communication panel + emergency button (wristband, necklace, or bijuteria). Range: 300m. Waterproof (IP67). Optional desmaio (fall/faint) sensor. Optional wall-mount voice-activated button ("socorro").
- **Proteção Fora de Casa** — portable GoSafe mobile pendant with GPS, 4G, fall sensor built-in, two-way voice, 36h battery.
- **Acompanhamento de Saúde** — optional health monitoring (vital signs) with personalized care plan.

## Differentiation

Brazil's largest emergency-monitoring operator, differentiated by an in-house medical monitoring centre staffed with nurses and doctors (not just dispatchers), a comprehensive Portuguese-language PERS platform, and a unique combination of in-home + mobile GPS protection with optional health monitoring and ambulance add-ons.

## Strengths

- ✅ Claims largest PERS operator in Brazil — 17+ years of brand equity and recognition.
- ✅ In-house Central Médica de Monitoramento with nurse+doctor+specialist staffing — clinical, not just logistical.
- ✅ Dual in-home + mobile GPS product line — covers users who stay home and those who go out.
- ✅ Smart features: voice-activated wall button ("socorro"), bijuteria (discreet jewelry-style button), automatic fall detection, onboard 4G chip with no user SIM management.
- ✅ Affordable entry pricing — `R$ 289–320/mês` (~$55 USD) is competitive for Brazilian middle-class families.
- ✅ Distribution via associations like AFPESP creates a recurring, trust-based acquisition channel.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ No visible caregiver mobile app — family notifications handled through the call centre by phone.
- ❌ Equipment is contract-based (adesão price signals retention lock-in) — not as friendly as device-purchase models.
- ❌ No international coverage — Portuguese/Spanish-only, no cross-border functionality for families living abroad.
- ❌ Pricing opaqueness beyond entry level — ambulance add-on, health monitoring upgrade not priced publicly.
- ❌ No smartwatch form factor — bracelet-style is conventional, not competitive with newer wearable-native offerings.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Caregiver app as differentiator** — Brazilian families are heavy WhatsApp users. A companion app with geolocation, check-in history, and instant messaging would leapfrog TeleHelp's phone-call-based notification model.
- **Device-purchase model** — offer ownership instead of adesão/contract equipment, appealing to families who don't want long-term financial commitments.
- **International-family dashboard** — millions of Brazilian adult children work abroad (US, Portugal, Japan); a multi-language remote-monitoring dashboard that bridges distance is an unmet need.
- **Network-effect referral** — the AFPESP association model shows that closed-group discount programs work in Brazil. MyLifeLine could replicate this through church groups, comunidades de idosos, and health cooperatives.
