## Differentiation

Brazil's self-claimed pioneer in teleassistência (23+ years), differentiated by zero joining fees, a 30-day cancellation guarantee, and the industry's fastest claimed answer time (até 7 segundos / within 7 seconds). Combines automatic fall detection with weekly wellness calls in a single plan.

## Strengths

- ✅ 23+ years in the Brazilian market — deep domestic experience and brand trust.
- ✅ Zero adesão (joining fee) removes the biggest friction to sign-up versus TeleHelp's R$599 adesão.
- ✅ Claimed sub-7-second answer time — a meaningful operational metric that competitors don't publish.
- ✅ Weekly wellness call (ligação de bem-estar) included as standard — proactive care, not just reactive.
- ✅ Device works inside and outside the home with GPS — single SKU simplicity vs separate in-home/mobile products.
- ✅ 30-day cancellation guarantee reduces adoption anxiety.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ No transparent pricing — all plans require contact-to-sales, unlike TeleHelp which at least publishes starting price.
- ❌ Limited product-line depth — single device (pendant/wristband), no dedicated in-home base, no smartwatch, no institutional products.
- ❌ 200,000 "atendimentos" is a service-event count, not a subscriber base — may be much smaller than TeleHelp.
- ❌ No caregiver mobile app visible — relies on phone-call notifications to family.
- ❌ No health-monitoring add-on (vital signs, chronic disease monitoring) — purely emergency response.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Transparent self-serve pricing** — Viva hides behind contact-to-sales; MyLifeLine could win with a clear online pricing table and digital sign-up.
- **Multi-generational caregiver app** — families want a dashboard to see location, check-in history, and wellness logs; neither Viva nor TeleHelp provides this.
- **Health-monitoring integration** — TeleHelp offers optional health monitoring; Viva does not. A PERS + vital-sign tracking combo at a clear price would stand out.
- **International caregiver interface** — same expat-family opportunity for Brazilian diaspora (US, Portugal, Japan) monitoring parents in Brazil.
