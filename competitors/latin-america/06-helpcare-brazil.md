## Product lineup

- **Botão HelpCare (In-Home)** — pendant/necklace/wristband emergency button. Key features:
  - 7-year battery (no recharging needed), waterproof, hypoallergenic
  - Two-way voice via home base console
  - Automatic fall detection (sensor de queda)
  - Works during power outages (battery backup in base)
  - Periodic automatic self-tests
  - Lifetime warranty (garantia vitalícia) — unusual for this market
- **i-Help (Mobile)** — mobile GPS pendant with geolocation, companion family app for tracking location and route.
- **Smart Watch Inteligente** — smartwatch-style device with GPS tracking, heart-rate monitoring, step/counter, medication reminders, and two-way voice.

## Differentiation

Unique among Brazilian PERS providers in offering a lifetime warranty on equipment, a 7-year non-rechargeable battery, and a three-device line-up spanning classic pendant, mobile GPS tracker, and smartwatch. The lifetime hardware guarantee eliminates a recurring anxiety point for families worried about broken/replaced devices.

## Strengths

- ✅ Lifetime warranty (garantia vitalícia) — no other Brazilian competitor makes this claim.
- ✅ 7-year non-rechargeable battery in the in-home pendant — zero maintenance for the senior user.
- ✅ Three-device portfolio: classic pendant, mobile GPS tracker (i-Help), and smartwatch (Smart Watch Inteligente).
- ✅ Caregiver mobile app with location tracking and route history (GPS models) — more tech-forward than TeleHelp or Viva.
- ✅ Auto-fall detection + two-way voice + power-outage backup — full PERS feature set.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ No transparent pricing anywhere on the website — contact-to-sales only.
- ❌ Company age and subscriber base are completely opaque — no founding year, no customer count, no social proof.
- ❌ Smart Watch Inteligente undefined — unclear if it's a white-label product or custom hardware.
- ❌ Website is less polished than TeleHelp's — potential trust gap for a service handling medical emergencies.
- ❌ The "lifetime warranty" claim is unbacked by visible terms/conditions — could be perceived as marketing hyperbole.
