## Differentiation

Mexico's largest private pre-hospital mobile ICU fleet with 50+ Mercedes-Benz ALS units and specialist physicians who come to the scene — not just paramedic-staffed transport. More akin to a private 911 service than a PERS wearable.

## Strengths

- ✅ 28+ years of experience in pre-hospital emergency medicine across multiple Mexican cities.
- ✅ 50+ properly-equipped mobile ICUs (Terapia Intensiva Móvil) — true ALS capability, not just BLS transport.
- ✅ Physician-staffed (Médicos Especialistas en terapia intensiva y urgenciología) — higher clinical capability than paramedic-only services.
- ✅ Direct-to-consumer subscription model — individuals can subscribe without needing a health plan.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ Dispatched on demand only — no wearable, no automatic fall detection, no passive monitoring. User must call.
- ❌ No caregiver app or location tracking — families have no visibility during an event.
- ❌ Reactive, not proactive — does not prevent emergencies or provide wellness check-ins.
- ❌ Geographically limited to cities with own fleet coverage — no service in more remote or rural areas.
- ❌ Premium cost structure (50+ Mercedes-Benz fleet + physician salaries is high capex/opex).

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Bridge the gap** — combine EMME's dispatch model with a wearable PERS device that auto-triggers the response. EMME gets faster notification; MyLifeLine gets the clinical response for its hardware.
- **Wellness monitoring layer** — the PERS wearable could do daily check-ins and vitals, reducing unnecessary EMME dispatch events and improving triage.
- **Rural/underserved areas** — EMME's fleet is metro-focused. A mobile-GPS-first wearable paired with a broader network of local response contacts could serve areas the UTIMs never reach.
