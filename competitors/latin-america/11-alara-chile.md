## Differentiation

A Chilean-designed hardware startup selling discrete, jewelry-like panic buttons with a one-time purchase model — no monthly fees, no monitoring centre, no contract. The location-alert is SMS-based via the user's own phone. Distinct from medical-alert services; it's a personal-safety accessory that competes on aesthetics and price, not on emergency monitoring.

## Strengths

- ✅ No subscription — one-time purchase model eliminates the biggest adoption barrier.
- ✅ Discrete, aesthetic design — jewelry, keychain, scrunchie form factors. Users actually want to wear it.
- ✅ Waterproof (Senior model) — safe for shower use, where many senior falls occur.
- ✅ Chilean startup with strong product-market fit for an aging population (projected 1/3 of Chile >60 by 2050).
- ✅ App with 5-contact alerting, real-time location sharing for 24 hours during emergency mode.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ NO monitoring centre — alerts go to family/friends, not 24/7 trained operators. Entirely dependent on contact availability.
- ❌ No fall detection — must manually double-click. Useless if user is unconscious.
- ❌ Bluetooth tethered to phone — user must carry their phone with Bluetooth on at all times for the device to work.
- ❌ No two-way voice — SMS link only; contacts can't speak to the user through the device.
- ❌ Not a medical alert system — does not integrate with emergency services, telemedicine, or health monitoring.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Add a monitoring back-end** — MyLifeLine could offer a 24/7 monitoring service as an optional monthly add-on for ALARA-type devices, converting a peer-to-peer panic button into a true PERS.
