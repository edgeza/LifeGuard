## Product lineup

Asistronic is a hardware retailer specializing in nurse-call and patient-monitoring equipment. Its senior/GPS product line overlaps with PERS:

- **Botón impermeable SOS con GPS EVOTRACK** — 4G GPS pendant: SOS button, two-way voice, automatic fall detection (70%+ accuracy), IP67 waterproof, geofence alerts, medication reminders, real-time GPS tracking. Works with a CR SIM card (CLARO). No monthly service fee — user provides own SIM and sets up own contacts.
- **Reloj inteligente SOS con GPS GUARDIAN 2.0** — smartwatch-style SOS device: GPS tracking, heart-rate monitoring, SOS button, fall detection, IP67, supports up to 10 phone numbers. Same SIM-based model.
- **Sistema Multialert** — institutional wireless nurse-call system for hospitals, nursing homes (hardware sale).
- **SensoCama / SensoPiso** — bed/pressure-mat alarms for Alzheimer/dementia patients who should not leave bed unsupervised.
- **Botón de llamado SOS remoto WiFi** — WiFi-based panic button for in-home use with app notifications.

## Differentiation

Colombia's largest assistive-technology retailer offering a unique hardware-only distribution model — no subscriptions, no monitoring services. Positions GPS SOS pendants alongside nurse-call systems and hospital equipment, making it the go-to B2B and B2C supplier for senior-care hardware in Colombia.

## Strengths

- ✅ Dominant Colombian marketplace position in ayudas técnicas — strong brand recognition in the assisted-living sector.
- ✅ Hardware-only model — no ongoing costs, attractive to budget-conscious families and institutions.
- ✅ Deep product portfolio — GPS pendants, smartwatches, nurse-call systems, bed alarms, WiFi call buttons — covers multiple use cases.
- ✅ Institutional focus — sells to hospitals, nursing homes, and geriatric centres, giving it a B2B moat.
- ✅ Free caregiver tracking app — included with GPS devices, no subscription.
- ✅ LatAm reach — ships outside Colombia.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ NO monitoring service — devices alert family/caregivers directly, no 24/7 trained operator backing.
- ❌ SIM-card friction — user must acquire and manage a CLARO SIM card with an active plan (extra step, extra cost, extra confusion).
- ❌ No defined medical escalation — devices connect to personal contacts, not to emergency services (ambulancias, hospitals).
- ❌ Hardware warranty and support — unclear post-purchase support for consumer devices.
- ❌ Website reflects a distributor, not a service brand — consumers seeking "peace of mind" find a complex catalogue, not a reassuring service.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Add the monitoring layer** — Asistronic sells the hardware; MyLifeLine could provide the 24/7 monitoring subscription on top. A reseller partnership: buy Asistronic devices, add MyLifeLine monitoring.
- **Remove the SIM friction** — bundle a pre-provisioned SIM and data plan into the purchase, making the device truly "out of the box."
- **Consumer-friendly brand** — the Colombian market lacks a dedicated senior-PERS consumer brand. Asistronic is a hardware store. MyLifeLine can be the trusted "service" brand for families.
- **Institutional partnerships** — Asistronic's hospital/nursing-home relationships are a ready-made B2B distribution channel for a monitoring service overlay.
