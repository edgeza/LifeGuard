## Differentiation

Mexican-owned teleassistance company combining European-assistive-technology hardware with a locally-staffed, solar-powered call centre in León Gto. The only visible Mexican competitor with transparent, published pricing in MXN — a significant trust signal in a market where competitors hide prices behind call-to-sales.

## Strengths

- ✅ Transparent, published pricing in MXN with three billing options (monthly/semester/annual).
- ✅ Resilient infrastructure — solar panels, battery backup, dedicated internet, and redundant telephony at the call centre.
- ✅ Dedicated focus on adultos mayores (seniors); 10+ years operating in this specific niche.
- ✅ Included medical reminders and follow-up calls (llamadas de seguimiento) as standard.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ Strictly in-home — no mobile GPS pendant for outside-the-house protection.
- ❌ Hardware is consignación (returned at end of contract), not owned — adds friction versus providers that allow purchase.
- ❌ No caregiver mobile app mentioned — family relies on call centre for updates, no self-serve tracking/dashboard.
- ❌ No fall-detection sensor — user must consciously press the button.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Mobile GPS add-on** — Cuida Más has no outside-the-home solution. MyLifeLine could partner or compete by offering an add-on mobile pendant to its base.
- **Incorporate ownership model** — allow device purchase instead of consignación, giving families a permanent asset.
- **Build a caregiver app** — Cuida Más's call-centre model ignores the app-native experience many Mexican families (especially expat children) expect.
- **English-language expansion** — no Mexican teleassistance competitor serves the English-speaking expat retiree market (San Miguel de Allende, Lake Chapala, Puerto Vallarta, etc.).
