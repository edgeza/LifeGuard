## Product lineup

- **App de Teleasistencia** — smartphone app (iOS and Android) with:
  - Botón de emergencia (panic button)
  - Botón "Estoy bien" (I'm OK check-in button)
  - Protocolo de ausencia (absence protocol — if user doesn't check in, alert is sent)
  - Aviso a contactos de emergencia (emergency contact notification)
  - Llamado de cortesía (courtesy call service)
  - Recordatorio de medicación (medication reminders)
  - Campaña de prevención (prevention campaigns)
- No hardware — entirely app-based on user's smartphone.

## Differentiation

A non-profit, smartphone-app-based teleassistance service from Argentina's Red Cross — no hardware, no dedicated monitoring centre, no GPS pendant. Focused on providing a low-cost, accessible safety net to seniors through an app they load on their own phone. Brand trust from the Red Cross is the primary differentiator.

## Strengths

- ✅ Red Cross (Cruz Roja) brand — the most trusted health/safety brand in Argentina and globally.
- ✅ No hardware barrier — accessible to any senior with a smartphone; lower cost structure.
- ✅ Well-designed app feature set: medication reminders, absence protocol, wellness check-in button — proactive, not just reactive.
- ✅ Non-profit pricing — probably the lowest-cost teleassistance option in Argentina.
- ✅ Nationwide coverage — no geographic hardware/cellular constraints.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ No fall detection — entirely manual push-button activation.
- ❌ App-only — excludes seniors who don't use smartphones or who experience cognitive decline.
- ❌ No dedicated monitoring centre — alerts go to family contacts, not trained operators. Response time depends on family availability.
- ❌ No GPS tracking — location accuracy depends on phone's GPS, which may not be worn consistently.
- ❌ Not suitable for fragile/fall-risk seniors who need passive monitoring.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Bridge the hardware gap** — MyLifeLine could partner with Cruz Roja to add a dedicated PERS pendant to the app ecosystem, keeping the Red Cross brand but adding the missing hardware layer.
- **Device-subsidy model** — Cruz Roja's non-profit reach could be the distribution channel; MyLifeLine's hardware the product. Joint "tech + trust" offering.
- **Tiered pricing from low to high** — Cruz Roja's low-cost app base means there's an upgrade path to dedicated hardware for families who can afford it.
- **Rural Argentina** — Cruz Roja's branch network reaches areas commercial PERS operators can't economically cover.
