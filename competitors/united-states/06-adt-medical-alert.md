## Differentiation

ADT extends its 150-year US home-security monitoring infrastructure into medical alerts with no equipment fee and a "ForeverRate" monthly-rate lock. It is the only listed US PERS where the operator is on the stock market (NYSE: ADT) and can demonstrate long-term institutional backing to consumers and benefit brokers. PERS is a small SKU in the larger ADT security business, not the company's lead category.

## Strengths

- ✅ 150-year brand trust — ADT's American District Telegraph was founded in 1874; second-most-recognizable security brand after the US government ⚠️.
- ✅ ForeverRate rate-lock — contract language that prevents future price increases is rare in the US PERS market.
- ✅ No equipment fee — the device is leased; only activation fee is $99 (one-time, all plans).
- ✅ Owned, UL-certified US 24/7 monitoring centres — no offshore routing.
- ✅ Lifetime warranty baked into the Premium Protection Plan (no additional cost).
- ✅ Cross-sell from ADT's 6 million security subscribers is a low-cost customer-acquisition channel.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ $99 activation fee on every plan — competitors (Bay Alarm Medical, Medical Guardian, MobileHelp) charge zero.
- ❌ No dedicated caregiver app — explicit consumer-review complaint (cf. elderlivinghub.com 2026 review).
- ❌ Three device SKUs only — fewer than Bay Alarm (6) or Medical Guardian (7).
- ❌ PERS is a small product line inside a large security company — limited product-roadmap agility vs specialist PE-backed challengers.
- ❌ Limited wholesale distribution (no Medicare Advantage partnerships advertised at scale).
- ❌ Customer-service reviews mixed; consistent with ADT's broader home-security reputation.
