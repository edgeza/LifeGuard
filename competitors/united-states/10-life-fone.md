## Product lineup

- **At-Home Landline** — base + pendant, $24.95/mo (annual prepay).
- **At-Home Cellular** — base + pendant (no landline needed), $30.95/mo (annual).
- **On-the-Go** — mobile GPS pendant, $39.95/mo (annual).
- **On-the-Go VIPx** — mobile GPS with caregiver app + 16-day battery, $41.95/mo.
- **Safe Watch** — LifeFone smartwatch, $44.95/mo; fall detection +$5/mo; VIPx2 caregiver app +$8/mo.

## Strengths

- ✅ Lowest published fall-detection add-on — +$5/mo vs $9-$11/mo competitors (Lively $9.99, MobileHelp $11).
- ✅ Lifetime price and equipment guarantee — "we'll never raise your rate, and we'll service or replace the equipment for life."
- ✅ Free spouse monitoring on At-Home plans — family-friendly add.
- ✅ Long industry tenure — 1976 founding, ~50 years; depth in PERS operations.
- ✅ VIPx long battery life (16 days) — strongest published battery metric in the US mobile-PERS category.
- ✅ A+ BBB rating; NCOA-partner reviews consistently favourable.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ Caregiver app costs extra ($8/mo VIPx2) — MyGuardian / Bay Alarm Caregiver are included free in base price.
- ❌ Older digital brand identity vs Bay Alarm / Medical Guardian / Lively — dated web UX.
- ❌ Smaller social-media footprint; lower PR velocity vs the PE-backed challengers.
- ❌ Limited device refresh cadence — fewer SKUs than Bay Alarm Medical or Medical Guardian.
- ❌ No smartwatch running Apple Watch Fall Detection API.
- ❌ No Spanish-language primary support visible.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Match the $5 fall-detection add-on.** LifeFone is the price leader on the most-upgraded PERS feature; MyLifeLine should match or include fall detection at no extra cost.
- **Caregiver app at no extra cost.** Include VIPx-style data dashboards in base subscription; the $8/mo add-on is a point of consumer friction.
- **Modernize the digital experience.** LifeFone's web UX signals "small family business"; a modern checkout and progressive-web-app feel wins millennial buyers.
- **Lifetime price-lock guarantee.** Match LifeFone's price-lock language in MyLifeLine's terms — a copy point for switchers.
