## Product lineup

- **Noonlight consumer app** — iOS + Android; press-and-hold button; release with PIN to cancel; release without PIN to dispatch.
- **Timeline feature** — share safety details with first responders.
- **Safety Network** — friends/family can request check-ins.
- **Automatic Crash Response** — uses accelerometer, GPS, gyroscope to detect car crashes.
- **Apple Watch + Wear OS apps** — wrist-based alarm.
- **Smart-home integrations** — Alexa, Google Home, IFTTT (Wyze, Nest Protect, Abode, Arlo, Scout, Canary).
- **Noonlight Household** — multi-user smart-home monitoring.
- **Noonlight API / Dispatcher-as-a-Service** — sold to smart-home companies, dating apps, ride-share apps to white-label emergency dispatch.

## Differentiation

Noonlight is the **consumer-grade personal-safety app with the largest brand and the strongest white-label emergency-dispatch platform** — the consumer app is free and works with Tinder/Lyft, while the **Dispatcher-as-a-Service API** lets other apps (Tinder, Wyze, smart-home vendors) white-label the dispatch layer. This is the most direct existing example of a **"personal-safety-as-a-service"** platform that MyLifeLine can learn from.

## Strengths

- ✅ **Free consumer tier** — broadens the addressable market to anyone with a smartphone.
- ✅ **Strong brand recognition** — "Protected by Noonlight" badge on Tinder, Lyft, Uber; ubiquitous among US consumers.
- ✅ **Noonlight Dispatcher-as-a-Service API** — proven white-label emergency-dispatch platform; few competitors offer this.
- ✅ **Tinder / Lyft / Uber / smart-home partnerships** — embedded in user flows where safety anxiety is acute.
- ✅ **iOS widget + Apple Watch + Wear OS + crash detection** — multi-surface activation.
- ✅ **Free 30-day trial for paid tiers** — low procurement friction.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ **US-only** — does not work internationally; major blocker for global enterprise buyers.
- ❌ **No enterprise / B2B dashboard** — consumer-grade; not a fit for organisations needing a manager console for 500+ workers.
- ❌ **No fall detection** (only crash detection).
- ❌ **No indoor location, no gas detection, no satellite, no mass-notification to fleet.**
- ❌ **No ARC partnerships disclosed** — appears to run its own dispatchers.
- ❌ **iOS gets features first** — Android users get less functionality.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **The Dispatcher-as-a-Service playbook is the MyLifeLine model.** Noonlight proves that consumer-grade personal-safety-as-a-service + white-label emergency-dispatch is a viable product. MyLifeLine can build the same model globally (Noonlight is US-only).
- **Multi-region + multi-language white-label.** Noonlight's API is English/US-only; MyLifeLine can offer the same white-label API in EN/ES/FR/AU/DE.
- **Cross-region certified dispatch.** Noonlight's dispatchers are US-based; MyLifeLine can partner with regional ARCs (UK EN 50518, AU ASIAL A1, EU DATI) to offer multi-jurisdiction compliance.
