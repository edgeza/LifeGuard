## Differentiation

AlertMedia's positioning is the **"single-pane-of-glass" between mass notification, threat intelligence, and lone-worker safety** — most mass-notification vendors (Everbridge, OnSolve) handle comms well but treat worker safety as a bolt-on, while pure-play lone-worker vendors (SoloProtect, Ok Alone) lack the comms scale to alert a 50,000-employee enterprise in 90 seconds.

## Strengths

- ✅ **Owned mass-notification infrastructure** — channels into SMS/voice/email/push/Slack/Teams, with 10-second throughput per Vendr benchmarks.
- ✅ **Strong threat-intelligence feed** — global incidents, weather, civil unrest, integrated into the alert console.
- ✅ **Modern UX.** Repeatedly cited as the easiest-to-deploy mass-notification product vs Everbridge's heavier enterprise stack.
- ✅ **Lone-worker app is a free module within the platform** — attractive for organisations that already pay for the mass-notification subscription.
- ✅ **No setup fees published** — lowers procurement friction.
- ✅ **SOC 2 + ISO 27001** — meets enterprise security bar.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ **No owned ARC.** Customers must run their own safety desk or buy third-party monitoring on top.
- ❌ **Expensive at scale.** Vendr observed $30K-$300K+ annual contracts; SMB pricing of $15K+ is double what a SMB would pay for Ok Alone or StaySafe.
- ❌ **No dedicated hardware SKU** — no gas detection, no dedicated wearable, no satellite option (only smartphone app).
- ❌ **Lone-worker feature set is shallower than dedicated vendors** — lacks BS 8484 / EN 50518 ARC URN capability that SoloProtect and Peoplesafe sell as a procurement requirement.
- ❌ **Annual escalator clauses (3-5%)** common in multi-year deals — finance teams push back on this (per Vendr).
