## Differentiation

My Safety Buddy is the **community-services-focused lone-worker app in ANZ** — positioning itself as the cheaper, simpler alternative to SafeTCard and Duress for community workers, real-estate agents, and utility crews who need A1-grade monitoring without paying enterprise prices.

## Strengths

- ✅ **Grade A1 ARC option** — same accreditation tier as SafeTCard and Duress.
- ✅ **Shake-to-activate emergency** — discreet and fast; matches Duress's mobile-UX quality.
- ✅ **Self-monitored entry tier** — cheaper than A1-monitored; broadens the buyer pool.
- ✅ **Australia data sovereignty** — fits AU/NZ data-residency requirements.
- ✅ **Wearable BLE duress buttons** — wrist, pocket, pendant form factors for different roles.
- ✅ **Strong community-services positioning** — non-profits, social workers, real estate are growth segments.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ **Limited brand recognition outside ANZ.**
- ❌ **Limited public pricing** — quote-only friction.
- ❌ **No indoor Bluetooth location** like CriticalArc or TraknProtect.
- ❌ **No gas detection, no satellite option, no dedicated hardware** — high-risk remote roles still need Blackline / SoloProtect.
- ❌ **No mass-notification** — needs AlertMedia / RAYVN cross-sell.
- ❌ **No BS 8484 / EN 50518 equivalent** — UK / EU regulated buyers face accreditation gaps.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Multi-region Grade A1 / EN 50518 ARC.** MyLifeLine can offer the same accreditation tier across multiple jurisdictions (UK + EU + AU + US) where each regional competitor is single-region.
- **Self-monitored tier for sub-25-seat accounts.** Many community-services NFPs and real-estate agencies want basic safety without paying ARC fees; MyLifeLine's free / low-cost self-monitored tier is the wedge.
- **Cross-region white-label.** ANZ competitors can't easily white-label outside their home market; MyLifeLine's global SaaS architecture is the wedge.
