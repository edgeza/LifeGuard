# Lone-Worker / Enterprise Safety — Competitive Landscape Summary

> **Scope:** 27 vendor profiles covering the **verified 8** (Blackline Safety, SoloProtect, StaySafe, AlertMedia, Everbridge, CriticalArc, Alertus, Singlewire) plus **19 additional** vendors researched and profiled.
> **Last refreshed:** 2026-07-17
> **Author context:** MyLifeLine competitor dossier — prepared to identify the **white-label opportunity** for MyLifeLine as a multi-tenant safety platform.

## How to read this document

The lone-worker / enterprise safety segment is structurally different from the senior PERS segment covered in `united-states/`. The buyer is an **employer**, not a consumer; the procurement decision is made by a **safety manager, HR, or risk officer**; the contracts are **annual or multi-year SaaS**, often quote-only with volume discounts; and the regulatory regime (BS 8484 in the UK, EN 50518 for EU ARCs, OSHA + state laws in the US, ASIAL A1 in Australia) creates **high switching costs** because once an Alarm Receiving Centre is BS 8484-accredited for a customer, replacing them requires re-accreditation.

MyLifeLine's white-label opportunity must therefore be evaluated against three vectors that matter more than feature parity:

1. **Multi-region accreditation parity** — can MyLifeLine offer BS 8484 / EN 50518 / UL-827 / TMA-Five-Diamond / ASIAL-A1 grade monitoring across multiple jurisdictions in one product, when most competitors are single-region?
2. **Multi-tier distribution** — can MyLifeLine serve the 1-25-seat self-monitored customer (where Ok Alone, The Sentry, StaySafe compete) **and** the 1,000+ seat enterprise (where AlertMedia, Everbridge compete) **and** the B2B channel partner (where Noonlight's Dispatcher-as-a-Service proves the model)?
3. **App-first with hardware-agnostic ARC** — most competitors lock to a single device or app; MyLifeLine's white-label advantage is being able to consume SOS signals from Blackline devices, Garmin inReach, SafeTCard pendants, Apple/Android apps, and third-party B2B partners through a single monitoring + manager layer.

## White-label opportunity ranking — top 5 plays for MyLifeLine

Based on the competitive matrix, these are the **five highest-leverage white-label opportunities** for MyLifeLine, ranked by addressable market × competitive gap × MyLifeLine's execution fit:

### 1. **Multi-region ARC-as-a-Service** (largest play)

**Wedge:** SoloProtect, Peoplesafe, Vatix, SafeTCard, Duress, My Safety Buddy, Guardian Angel Safety, and My Keeper each own or partner with a **single-region ARC** (UK EN 50518, AU ASIAL A1, FR DATI, etc.). Most enterprise customers want a single monitoring contract that works in multiple jurisdictions — Everbridge and AlertMedia sell software, not ARC. **Noonlight's Dispatcher-as-a-Service** is the only true multi-region DaaS player, but is US-only.

**MyLifeLine play:** Build a **multi-tenant SaaS that partners with regional ARCs** (UK EN 50518, EU EN 50518, AU ASIAL A1, US TMA-Five-Diamond) and offers a unified "ARC-as-a-Service" product. Customers can choose their jurisdiction's ARC and get the same MyLifeLine app + dashboard. Targets the global enterprise that buys from Peoplesafe UK + SafeTCard AU separately today.

**Target customers:** multinational enterprise with dispersed workforces, multinational staffing agencies, global hotel chains, global retailers, multinational utilities.

### 2. **SMB self-monitored tier + ARC upgrade** (fastest to ship)

**Wedge:** The Sentry (£39.50/year, no ARC), Ok Alone ($10/month Essential), StaySafe (£3.30/month on UK G-Cloud), and Zecure.ai's price-disruptor positioning prove that **a transparent, low-cost app-only tier with optional ARC upgrade** is a viable product. Above that, SoloProtect, Peoplesafe, and Vatix sell enterprise pricing that excludes 1-25-seat customers.

**MyLifeLine play:** Ship a **self-monitored free or £5/month tier** for sub-25-seat customers with full app + manager console + Wi-Fi indoor positioning + privacy-first architecture. Offer an ARC upgrade tier at £15-£30/user/month. Use the same platform for both tiers; tier gating via features and ARC routing rather than separate products.

**Target customers:** small care agencies, real-estate offices, individual social workers, community-services NFPs, small hotel/hospitality properties.

### 3. **White-label mobile panic button for mass-notification incumbents** (highest B2B2B leverage)

**Wedge:** AlertMedia, Everbridge, Singlewire, Alertus, and RAYVN all sell multi-channel mass notification. **None of them own a strong mobile panic-button app** — they integrate with third-party mobile apps or build thin in-house apps. The mobile lone-worker market is dominated by SoloProtect / StaySafe / Peoplesafe; the mass-notification market is dominated by AlertMedia / Everbridge / Singlewire.

**MyLifeLine play:** Offer MyLifeLine's lone-worker mobile app as a **white-label mobile panic button backend** that AlertMedia, Everbridge, Singlewire, Alertus, and RAYVN can integrate via REST API. Each of these vendors has customer RFPs that ask "and what about our lone workers?" — MyLifeLine is the answer they resell under their own brand.

**Target customers:** AlertMedia / Everbridge / Singlewire / Alertus / RAYVN themselves (white-label channel), plus their enterprise customers (co-branded).

### 4. **Vertical pre-configured safety stack** (highest gross margin)

**Wedge:** SoloProtect and StaySafe sell horizontal lone-worker; CriticalArc and TraknProtect are vertical-specialist but limited to higher-ed and hospitality respectively. **No vendor offers pre-configured vertical packages** for the highest-volume verticals (home healthcare, social services, hospitality, retail, construction, logistics, security, education).

**MyLifeLine play:** Build **vertical templates** that ship with pre-configured feature sets, regulatory documentation, training content, hardware SKUs, and integration connectors per vertical. For example: a "Home Healthcare" tier with pre-built Apple/Android apps, MyLifeLine BLE wearables, eMAR integration, US state-by-state compliance documentation, training videos. Same architecture, different config per vertical.

**Target customers:** franchise networks, vertical-specialist resellers, vertical-industry conferences and trade shows.

### 5. **Privacy-first / unionised-worker tier** (highest brand differentiator)

**Wedge:** Duress (AU) and The Sentry (UK) have validated that **"only-on-alarm" location tracking** is a differentiator in worker-surveillance-sensitive verticals (community workers, social workers, unions, government). Most competitors require ongoing tracking for the manager console to work.

**MyLifeLine play:** Ship a **"privacy-first" tier** with location only shared during active emergencies or working sessions, no background tracking, GDPR/CCPA-compliant by default, with on-device storage of working-session history. This is technically feasible (mirrors Duress's privacy architecture) and is a real marketing wedge in EU markets and unionised North American workforces.

**Target customers:** public-sector unions, EU healthcare workers, AU/NZ community-services NFPs, UK local-government social workers.

## Strategic recommendation

**MyLifeLine's best path is play #1 (multi-region ARC-as-a-Service) + play #3 (white-label mobile panic button for mass-notification incumbents), executed in parallel.**

- **Play #1** captures the global enterprise that today buys multiple regional vendors (Peoplesafe UK + SafeTCard AU + SoloProtect + Blackline, etc.) and consolidates them into one platform.
- **Play #3** captures the AlertMedia / Everbridge / Singlewire / Alertus / RAYVN customer base that asks for lone-worker and gets pointed at SoloProtect or StaySafe today.

Together, the two plays give MyLifeLine a **clear differentiation story** that no current vendor can match: **one platform, one app, one contract, multi-region ARC, multi-tenant SaaS, white-label-ready, hardware-agnostic.** That combination is the white-label opportunity MyLifeLine should lead its go-to-market with.
