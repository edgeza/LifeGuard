## Product lineup

- **SafeZone** — flagship unified safety, security, wellbeing and emergency-management platform.
- **SafeZone mobile app** — iOS + Android; single-button duress alert, two-way voice, geo-located check-in, mass communication, tip reporting, mental-health & wellbeing check-ins.
- **Omniguard** — real-time response coordination; ties dispatcher console to responder mobile app.
- **SafeTrans** — transport tracking for safety and security.
- **Indoor Positioning** — Bluetooth beacons + Wi-Fi fingerprinting for room/floor-level accuracy.
- **Mass and Targeted Communications** — broadcast to user groups, geofenced zones, floors.
- **Active Threat Management** — pre-defined scenarios; multi-channel broadcast.
- **Lone Worker Management** — high-risk and out-of-hours worker check-ins, optional integration with discrete Bluetooth wearables.

## Differentiation

CriticalArc's distinct position is **indoor location + distributed command-and-control** — SafeZone is the only product in this dossier that can show a dispatcher, in real-time, which floor and which room a duress alert originated from on a multi-building campus, and then coordinate first-responder movement on the same map. Higher-ed and hospital campuses are the primary buyer.

## Strengths

- ✅ **Indoor positioning capability** — Bluetooth + Wi-Fi fingerprinting; few lone-worker vendors match this depth.
- ✅ **Distributed command-and-control UI** — multiple dispatchers across multiple sites on one map; ideal for university multi-campus estates.
- ✅ **Public-sector procurement pre-clearance** — UK G-Cloud 11 and AU NSW ICT Services Scheme approval saves procurement cycles.
- ✅ **Education pricing + free trial** — removes the procurement-cost objection for universities and colleges.
- ✅ **Omniguard + SafeTrans** extend the product beyond lone worker into security operations and transport safety — broader TAM per customer.
- ✅ **Strong integration story** — HPE Aruba Meridian, Cisco IPICS, SSO; plugs into existing campus technology stacks.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ **Per-instance pricing** — £18,900/instance means a multi-campus university pays per site; adds up fast for national networks.
- ❌ **No owned ARC.** Customers self-monitor; not a fit for organisations without a 24/7 security operations centre.
- ❌ **No dedicated wearable hardware** — relies on smartphones (and optional Bluetooth buttons); high-risk roles still need Blackline or SoloProtect hardware.
- ❌ **No gas detection, no satellite option, no push-to-talk.**
- ❌ **Limited brand outside AU/UK higher-ed and healthcare** — not a household name in the US enterprise market.
- ❌ **Free trial but "services are chargeable to set-up the solution / provide training"** — on-boarding cost not transparent.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Multi-tenant indoor positioning for non-campus customers.** CriticalArc's indoor positioning is sold per instance; MyLifeLine can offer Bluetooth beacon + Wi-Fi fingerprinting as a feature of the standard SaaS plan, removing the per-site fee.
- **Plays in adjacent verticals.** CriticalArc is anchored in higher-ed and healthcare; MyLifeLine can take the same tech stack to logistics, manufacturing, retail, hospitality where indoor location is also valuable.
- **Disparity between ARC-less SafeZone and the mass market.** Most 50-500 employee companies don't run a 24/7 SOC; MyLifeLine can offer a managed-monitoring tier that SafeZone refuses to bundle.
