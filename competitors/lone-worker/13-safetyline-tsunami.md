## Differentiation

SafetyLine is the **lone-worker app + Garmin inReach satellite bridge** — a Canadian vertical-focused vendor that goes to market by saying "if you need satellite coverage for forestry / oil & gas / remote construction workers, we are the simplest path because we resell Garmin inReach and integrate it with our app and ARC". Few competitors in this dossier offer the satellite option as a packaged product.

## Strengths

- ✅ **Garmin inReach authorised reseller** — one of the few lone-worker vendors that sells + integrates + monitors satellite devices as a single SKU.
- ✅ **Strong vertical focus on remote-area industries** — forestry, oil & gas, water utilities, home healthcare.
- ✅ **App-first design** — easy onboarding for SMB customers.
- ✅ **Pricing transparency** — public-facing prices on the website (rare in this segment).
- ✅ **Canadian presence + OSHA + Canadian OHS alignment** — strong North America + AU/NZ reseller story.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ **Brand awareness outside Canada + AU/NZ is low.**
- ❌ **No indoor Bluetooth location** — limited for hospitality, healthcare, retail.
- ❌ **Outsourced ARC** — single point of failure for some regulated buyers.
- ❌ **No gas detection of its own** — must integrate with third-party gas monitors.
- ❌ **No mass-notification** — needs AlertMedia/Everbridge/RAYVN cross-sell.
- ❌ **Limited public API documentation** — integration friction for enterprise buyers.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Multi-region satellite resale.** Garmin inReach is global; MyLifeLine can build the same satellite-bundle SKU with broader region coverage than SafetyLine's AU/NZ + Canada focus.
- **Add mass-notification + ARC layer.** SafetyLine customers frequently ask "what else can you do for the rest of my team?" — MyLifeLine's broader platform is the answer.
- **Vertical-specific feature bundles** (forestry, oil & gas) — SafetyLine has the right vertical focus but a thin SKU; MyLifeLine can offer richer features at the same price.
