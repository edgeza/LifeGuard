## Product lineup

- **Alert Beacon** — wall-mounted strobe/speaker + text display; integrates with fire alarm, building management systems; CAN/ULC-S576 listed.
- **Alertus Desktop Notification** — software client for Windows + macOS; full-screen takeover alerts.
- **Desktop Activator** — software-based panic button on every workstation; hotkey + task-tray icon.
- **USB Panic Button** — discreet under-the-counter button; triggers alert when screen is locked.
- **Wall-mounted Activation Button** — physical duress button connected to the Alert Beacon or Alertus Server.
- **AlertAware mobile app** — iOS + Android; successor to the older Mobile Recipient App; panic-button activation; receive alerts.
- **Alertus Dispatcher** — mobile activator app for authorised responders to launch and cancel alerts.
- **VoIP Phone, IP Speaker, Digital Signage, LED Marquee** integrations — extends alerts to existing endpoints.
- **Text-to-Speech interface** for PA systems.

## Differentiation

Alertus is the **on-premises + hardware-rich** alternative to Everbridge and AlertMedia — whereas those vendors focus on software channels (SMS, voice, email, push), Alertus bets on **physical endpoints**: panic buttons, wall-mounted strobes, IP speakers, desktop takeover. That makes Alertus the **Alyssa-Law-compliant** choice for K-12 schools (NJ's Alyssa Law mandates panic buttons in classrooms) and a strong fit for higher-ed campus estates with existing fire-alarm and PA infrastructure.

## Strengths

- ✅ **Hardware-rich endpoint coverage** — panic buttons + Alert Beacons + IP speakers + digital signage + LED marquees; the only vendor with this depth in this dossier.
- ✅ **On-premises option** — fits regulated/government buyers that cannot use cloud-only CEM (FedRAMP-adjacent buyers, school districts with strict data-residency rules).
- ✅ **NFPA 72 + UL 2572 + FEMA IPAWS** integration — first-responder-grade certifications rare in the software-only competitors.
- ✅ **Alyssa-Law-compliant K-12 hardware** — major procurement advantage in New Jersey, expanding to other US states.
- ✅ **Desktop takeover** — full-screen alerts cannot be ignored the way SMS can; useful for active-threat scenarios.
- ✅ **Open integration architecture** — Cisco, Microsoft Teams, fire alarm panels, BMS, social media, RAVE/Mobile Alert.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ **No dedicated lone-worker wearable** — relies on AlertAware app on employee phones; no Blackline-style device.
- ❌ **No ARC.** Customers self-monitor; for 24/7 safety monitoring you must pair with SoloProtect/Peoplesafe.
- ❌ **Hardware-driven project cost.** Beacon + button + IP speaker installations can run six figures for a large campus.
- ❌ **Less elegant UX** — Alertus is a control-room product, not an end-user app; mobile app is functional but not best-in-class.
- ❌ **Limited brand recognition outside the US education + government niche.**
- ❌ **Implementation timeline is months, not weeks** — fire-panel integration and BMS work slow rollouts.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Plug in below Alertus's hardware ceiling.** Many small schools and mid-sized healthcare networks can't afford Alertus's hardware-first deployments; MyLifeLine's app-first product is the lower-cost alternative.
