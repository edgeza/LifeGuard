# Age UK Personal Alarms & Age Co Personal Alarms

- **Websites**: 
  - https://personalalarms.ageuk.org.uk (Age UK branded storefront)
  - https://personalalarms.ageco.co.uk (Age Co branded storefront)
  - https://trading.ageuk.org.uk/personal-alarms/ (Age UK Trading)
- **Brand Owner**: Age UK Trading Ltd / Age Co Ltd (commercial trading subsidiaries of Age UK, the national charity)
- **Operational Provider**: **Taking Care (AXA Health subsidiary)** — both brands are white-label contracts run by Taking Care
- **Charity Partner**: Age UK (registered charity 1128267)

## Business Model
**B2C, white-label / charity-affiliated retail**. Customers buy through the Age UK Trading / Age Co storefront, but fulfilment, ARC monitoring, billing, and customer service are operated by Taking Care. Age UK Trading audits Taking Care's product and procedures annually.

## Reach
Age UK is **the** most recognised brand for older people in the UK (charity brand since 1970). The personal-alarm service has supported **¼ million+ people and their families** per Taking Care's claims. Both sites run on Shopify + Shopify-quiz-builder.

## Pricing Differentiator
**Age Co is consistently £2-3 cheaper per month** than Age UK Personal Alarms for the same product (likely because Age Co is a separate commercial brand without full charity royalty overhead).

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Age UK charity brand** = unmatched trust with elderly UK consumers (10x stronger than any MyLifeLine brand awareness in UK)
- "Dementia Friendly" organisation status appeals to families
- Annual audit by Age UK Trading gives credibility
- VAT exemption handled, no-quibble refunds, 30-day money-back
- Operationally mature 24/7 UK-based ERCs

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- **Not a separate product company** — these are Taking Care white-labels. MyLifeLine has its own panic-button hardware + platform
- Pendant/home-centric (vs MyLifeLine's mobile GPS pendant model)
- No white-label SaaS / reseller partner program (they ARE a white-label themselves)
- South African brand origin gives MyLifeLine no advantage here; UK consumer wants UK charity brand
