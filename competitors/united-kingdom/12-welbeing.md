# Welbeing (Wealden and Eastbourne Lifeline)

- **Website**: https://www.welbeing.org.uk
- **Headquarters**: Greencoat House, 32 St Leonards Road, Eastbourne, East Sussex, BN21 3UT (operational base: Moy Avenue, Eastbourne BN22 8LD)
- **Founded**: Created from merger of Wealden District Council Lifeline + Eastbourne Borough Council Lifeline (~20 years each); spun out as independent not-for-profit

## Business Model
**B2C primary + B2G** — sells direct to private individuals (from £3.68/week) and provides TEC services to local authorities and housing associations nationwide.

## Core Products
- **Lifeline pendant + base unit** — from **£3.68/week** (one of UK's cheapest), + £37 one-off installation (covers all future maintenance)
- Wide range of telecare sensors (smoke, CO, fall, flood, bogus-caller, medication)
- 24/7/365 contact-centre response (East Sussex)
- Optional response service in local areas

## Reach
- "One of the fastest growing telecare organisations in the UK"
- Nationwide coverage; deeply rooted in East Sussex county council & housing-association ecosystem

## TSA Status
TSA member.

## Tech Stack
Tunstall-class dispersed alarm hardware; standard telecare peripherals.

## Strengths vs MyLifeLine
- **Not-for-profit / social-enterprise positioning** resonates with local-authority buyers
- **£3.68/week entry price** is the lowest monthly in our UK review set — undercuts every commercial competitor
- All-inclusive maintenance in the install fee — predictable cost
- Local Eastbourne response team can do physical lifts (similar to Progress Lifeline model)

## Weaknesses vs MyLifeLine
- No mobile GPS pendant with anywhere-coverage positioning
- No white-label SaaS / reseller programme
- Smaller brand than Careline365 / Taking Care / Age UK
- Limited international footprint
