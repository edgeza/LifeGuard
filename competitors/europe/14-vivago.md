## Product lineup

- **Vivago CARE watch** — wrist-worn wellbeing watch; SOS button + automatic fall/unconsciousness detection. Continuously measures activity, sleep, circadian rhythm. Worn on the same wrist for reliable data.
- **Vivago Vista software** — CE-marked medical-device software; collects + analyses alarms + wellbeing data. Real-time adaptive algorithms + sleep-measurement algorithms (co-developed with sleep researchers).
- **Care reports** — RAI (Resident Assessment Instrument) tracking via circadian rhythm.
- **Wandering control** — watch recognises user movement; alerts care staff if user leaves safe area.
- **Smart notifications** — automatic alarms sent to care team when wellness changes.

## Differentiation

Vivago's USP is the **medical-device-grade wellness analytics** — not just fall detection, but full circadian rhythm, sleep, and activity tracking that ties to RAI scores and care-effectiveness measurement. This is the only watch that gets a CE mark as a **medical device** (not just consumer wearable). It is more clinical/assessment-grade than Navigil, more proactive than Tunstall/TeleAlarm carephones. Direct competitors: Navigil 580, Apple Watch (wellness features), careium's wellness line.

## Strengths

- ✅ **CE-marked medical device** — only telecare watch with this status in Europe.
- ✅ Circadian rhythm + sleep analysis (vs SOS-only competitors).
- ✅ RAI-score correlation — directly informs care planning.
- ✅ 100k+ users in production.
- ✅ Clinical validation — sleep researchers co-developed the algorithms.
- ✅ Real-time adaptive algorithms.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ Watch form factor + base-station-dependent — limited outdoor/mobile use.
- ❌ No GPS — relies on base-station range.
- ❌ B2B only — no consumer brand.
- ❌ Wear compliance can be challenging (must be worn on same wrist for data integrity).
- ❌ Less known than Navigil outside Finland.
