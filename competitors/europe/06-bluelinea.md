## Product lineup

- **HELP (téléassistance à domicile)** — classic at-home bracelet + base station with GSM antenna (no landline required), speakerphone, 24/7 platform connection.
- **HELP Mobile (téléassistance mobile)** — GPS-enabled mobile pendant for outside-the-home use; geolocation.
- **HELP+ (détection de chutes)** — fall-detection bracelet; auto-alert on heavy fall.
- **HELP Visio** — home teleassistance with video camera.
- **HELP Access** — teleassistance for disabled persons (adapted equipment).
- **B2B "Connected Objects" service** — IoT/health-device monitoring (blood pressure, scales, glucometer) with professional caregiver dashboard.

## Differentiation

Bluelinea is the **largest French indépendant téléassistance pure-player**. Its "100% Made in France" (ARC in Versailles, equipment manufactured in France) and "sans engagement" (no-commitment) are the consumer messaging moats. Versus the social-services incumbent Croix-Rouge / Malteser (which is B2G-oriented and slower to innovate), Bluelinea is the consumer-friendly French operator. Versus Tunstall Televida (B2G operator), Bluelinea owns its customer relationship and the D2C billing.

## Strengths

- ✅ French consumer brand — strongest pure-player téléassistance brand in FR.
- ✅ Owned ARC — Versailles-based; 100% in-house operators; psychologist-supervised.
- ✅ Made in France positioning — culturally resonant with senior demographic.
- ✅ Sans engagement (no commitment) — unique vs French competition.
- ✅ Euronext-listed — capital access for expansion.
- ✅ Silver Alliance membership — cross-EU partnership reach.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ Hardware is sourced/relabelled — not a hardware innovator.
- ❌ France-centric — limited international footprint outside Belgium partnership.
- ❌ B2B channel development slower than Tunstall/CHT (Centrale d'Habitat et de Téléalarme network).
- ❌ Smaller revenue base than careium or Tunstall.
- ❌ Limited B2B SaaS for security-company resellers — competitors like Chubb/Verklizan offer this.
