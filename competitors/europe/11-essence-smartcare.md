## Product lineup

- **Care@Home PERS** — personal emergency response pendant + hub.
- **Care@Home PERS-E** — enhanced social alarm with cellular + IP connectivity.
- **Care@Home Family** — self-monitored family-care solution (no ARC required).
- **Care@Home Pro** — proactive AI-driven activity-monitoring with personalised alerts based on routine deviation.
- **Care@Home Activity Monitoring** — non-intrusive sensors (PIR, door, motion) that build user behaviour profile; raise alarms on abnormal patterns.
- **MDsense fall sensor** — radar-based, contactless fall detection (no wearable needed). Room-mounted. Cited as a paradigm shift.
- **Care@Home Multi-Service Platform** — cloud-based SaaS for service providers; multi-tenant; manages thousands of households.
- **Voice-activated alerts** — voice-extended emergency call.

## Differentiation

Essence SmartCare's biggest innovation is **MDsense — contactless radar-based fall detection** that works without a wearable. This is the most disruptive sensor-tech in EU telecare as of 2026. The Care@Home Pro AI-driven activity monitoring (alerts on routine deviation, not just falls) is also industry-leading. Direct competitors: Tunstall (Tunstall Smart Hub), TeleAlarm (TA74), Legrand Care (NOVO), Chiptech (SEVEN), Vivago. Versus all of them, Essence leads on AI/contactless fall detection.

## Strengths

- ✅ MDsense radar fall detection — no wearable required; paradigm shift.
- ✅ Care@Home Pro AI — proactive alerts, not just reactive SOS.
- ✅ Multi-tenant cloud platform — scales to thousands of households.
- ✅ 30+ country distribution.
- ✅ Multi-modal product line — PERS + Family + Pro + MDsense covers all segments.
- ✅ FocusCura (NL) and other large EU telecare players as reference customers.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ Israeli HQ — some EU customers prefer EU-based suppliers (GDPR / Schrems II / data-residency concerns).
- ❌ No owned ARC.
- ❌ B2B-only — no D2C consumer brand.
- ❌ Less brand recognition in DE/FR than TeleAlarm/Tunstall.
- ❌ Hardware-software platform is complex; integrators required for large deployments.
