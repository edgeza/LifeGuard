## Product lineup

- **Smart Care System (Komplettpaket)** — base station + 5 door/window sensors + 2 motion sensors + 1 radio trigger. Sensor-based AAL (ambient assisted living) detecting irregular day rhythms (missing morning wake-up, prolonged inactivity, unusual exits).
- **Gigaset E720 HX** — big-button DECT cordless phone with SOS button.
- **Gigaset Notrufknopf 100** — radio trigger (SOS pendant).
- **Gigaset Smart Care Cockpit** — web-based interface for professional service providers (carers, Hausnotruf operators) to manage multiple client households.
- **Optional accessories** — fall sensor, smoke detector, water sensor, extended PIR.

## Differentiation

Gigaset Smart Care is positioned as the "smart-home alternative to traditional Hausnotruf" — a sensor-based AAL system that detects abnormal routines without requiring the user to wear anything. Its 2024 strategic pivot from D2C to B2B makes it a direct competitor to Legrand Care and TeleAlarm at the "Hausnotruf 2.0" (sensor-plus-hub) layer. Its consumer phone brand (DECT + senior phones) gives it channel advantage with retail and telcos.

## Strengths

- ✅ DECT heritage — Germany/Europe's #1 cordless phone brand; "Made in Germany" production.
- ✅ Strong retail/telco distribution (Vodafone, Telekom, retail chains).
- ✅ Modular sensor system — 5+ sensor types out of the box; expandable.
- ✅ Smart Care Cockpit — purpose-built web interface for B2B operators managing fleets of households.
- ✅ 175-year corporate heritage (ancestor Siemens phone division).
- ✅ GDPR-compliant; German-hosted cloud; "Made in Germany" trust narrative.

## Weaknesses / gaps

- ❌ **Pivot away from D2C** — no consumer-direct purchase path; must go through a service partner.
- ❌ No GPS pendant / mobile solution — strictly fixed-home sensor play.
- ❌ No ARC of its own — relies on partner monitoring.
- ❌ No fall detection in core kit — accessory required.
- ❌ Hardware is stationary; ageing-cohort users increasingly expect mobile.
- ❌ Smaller player vs Legrand Care/TeleAlarm at the B2B-Hausnotruf interface.

## MyLifeLine opportunity

- **Mobile complement for the stationary cohort**: Gigaset serves fixed-home seniors. As those customers age into mobility, MyLifeLine's GPS pendant is a natural upsell — partner as the mobile SKU.
- **White-label reseller**: Gigaset's B2B pivot means they're now packaging Smart Care as a service partner program. MyLifeLine could be a sub-platform for Gigaset's reseller network (security companies, energy utilities, telcos).
- **Caregiver app depth**: Gigaset's app is single-household-focused. A multi-caregiver fleet app is open territory.
